# Attendance Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy outlines the attendance expectations, working hour standards, late coming guidelines, and absence management procedures for all employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Consistent and punctual attendance is essential for maintaining team productivity, meeting client commitments, and ensuring smooth operations across all offices. This policy ensures fairness and transparency in tracking and managing employee attendance.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all full-time permanent and probationary employees across all AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. offices in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai. Employees on approved remote work arrangements are also governed by this policy with adaptations as specified in the Work From Home Policy.

**3. Standard Working Hours**

The standard working hours at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, inclusive of a 1-hour unpaid lunch break. The net productive working hours expected per day are 8 hours. The standard working week consists of 5 days totalling 40 hours of productive work. Employees are expected to be at their workstations and ready to begin work by 9:00 AM. The core hours during which all employees must be available for meetings, collaboration, and client calls are 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Outside of core hours, employees with manager approval may exercise flexi-start options as described in Section 6.

**4. Attendance Tracking**

All employees are required to mark their attendance through the biometric system at office entry or through the AkhilaPrime HRMS mobile application for remote workdays. Attendance is tracked at two points: clock-in upon arrival and clock-out at the end of the working day. Failure to clock in or clock out will be treated as a half-day absence unless the employee notifies HR within the same day with a valid explanation. Employees must not mark attendance on behalf of another employee. Proxy attendance marking is a serious violation of this policy and will lead to immediate disciplinary action.

**5. Late Coming Policy**

Employees are expected to arrive at the workplace by 9:00 AM. A grace period of 15 minutes is allowed, meaning arrivals up to 9:15 AM will not be flagged. Arrivals between 9:16 AM and 10:00 AM will be recorded as late. Arrivals after 10:00 AM without prior intimation will be treated as a half-day absence. Three late arrivals in a calendar month will be treated as one half-day Loss of Pay deduction. Chronic late coming defined as more than 5 instances in a month will trigger a formal counselling session with the reporting manager and HR. Repeated patterns of late coming post-counselling may result in written warning or disciplinary action. Employees who anticipate being late due to unavoidable circumstances such as medical emergencies or public transport disruptions must notify their reporting manager via message or call before 9:15 AM.

**6. Flexi-Time Arrangement**

Employees may opt for a flexi-start time with prior written approval from their reporting manager. Approved flexi-time windows are 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (standard), or 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM. The core hours of 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM remain mandatory regardless of the chosen flexi window. Flexi-time arrangements are reviewed quarterly and can be revoked by the manager if project demands require it. Flexi-time does not apply during client visits, team events, or mandatory all-hands meetings.

**7. Absence Management**

Planned absences such as earned leave or casual leave must be applied for in advance through the HRMS portal with manager approval. Unplanned absences must be communicated to the reporting manager and HR via call or message before 9:30 AM on the day of absence. The employee must apply for appropriate leave on HRMS on the same day or the next working day. Absences not covered by approved leave will be automatically marked as Loss of Pay after 3 working days of unapplied absence. If an employee is absent for more than 3 consecutive days without intimation or leave approval, HR will attempt to contact the employee. If the employee remains unreachable for 7 consecutive working days without communication, the absence may be treated as abandonment of employment and may lead to termination.

**8. Half-Day Policy**

Employees may avail a half-day leave for personal requirements with manager approval. The first half is defined as 9:00 AM to 1:00 PM and the second half as 2:00 PM to 6:00 PM. A half-day will be deducted from the respective leave balance. Half-days must be applied through HRMS in advance or on the same day for emergencies.

**9. Overtime**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. does not have a standard overtime pay structure for its software and IT service employees who are categorised as knowledge workers. However, employees required to work beyond standard hours for extended project deliverables will be entitled to Compensatory Off as described in the Leave Policy. All overtime work must be pre-approved by the reporting manager in writing. Habitual reliance on overtime by a team may be reviewed by HR as a resource planning issue.

**10. Attendance and Payroll**

Monthly attendance records are reconciled with payroll by the 20th of every month. Any discrepancies must be raised by the employee through HRMS or directly with HR before the 22nd of the month. Leave without pay deductions are calculated on the basis of actual working days in the month. The per-day deduction rate for LOP is calculated as gross monthly salary divided by the number of working days in that month.

**11. Roles and Responsibilities**

Employees are responsible for clocking in and out accurately, applying for leave in advance, and notifying managers in case of unplanned absence. Reporting managers are responsible for monitoring team attendance, approving or rejecting leave requests within 2 working days, and escalating attendance concerns to HR. HR is responsible for maintaining attendance records, conducting periodic audits, and addressing systematic attendance issues.

**12. Policy Violations**

Violations of this policy including proxy attendance, habitual late coming, unapproved absences, or falsification of attendance records will be treated as misconduct. Depending on the severity, violations may result in verbal warning, written warning, salary deduction, or termination.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually. Amendments will be communicated to employees with a minimum of 30 days notice via the company intranet and official email.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees are required to acknowledge receipt and understanding of this policy upon joining and upon subsequent revisions through the HRMS portal.
