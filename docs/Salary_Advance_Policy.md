# Salary Advance Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy governs the process by which eligible employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. may request an advance on their salary in cases of genuine personal financial need. AkhilaPrime Solutions recognises that employees may occasionally face unexpected financial hardship and aims to provide a fair, structured, and interest-free mechanism to support them without burdening them with exploitative terms. This policy establishes eligibility criteria, advance limits, repayment terms, and the process for requesting and approving salary advances.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who have completed at least 12 months of continuous service from their date of confirmation. Probationary employees, contractual staff, interns, and consultants are not eligible for salary advances under this policy.

**3. Definition of Salary Advance**

A salary advance is a portion of an employee's future salary disbursed early by the company, to be recovered through subsequent payroll deductions over an agreed repayment period. It is not a loan, does not carry any interest, and is recovered entirely from the employee's own future salary. A salary advance is distinct from an emergency advance, which is a smaller, faster disbursement described in Section 7 of this policy.

**4. Eligibility Criteria**

To be eligible for a salary advance, the employee must meet all of the following conditions. The employee must have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous confirmed service with AkhilaPrime Solutions. The employee must be in active employment and not under notice period at the time of the request. The employee must not currently have an outstanding salary advance or emergency advance with the company. The employee must not be on a Performance Improvement Plan or under active disciplinary proceedings. The employee's previous salary advance, if any, must have been fully repaid for at least 6 months before a new advance request is made.

**5. Advance Limits**

The maximum salary advance an eligible employee may receive in a single application is as follows based on their band. Employees at Band 1 to Band 3 are eligible for an advance of up to 1 month's gross salary. Employees at Band 4 to Band 5 are eligible for an advance of up to 1.5 months' gross salary. Employees at Band 6 and above are eligible for an advance of up to 2 months' gross salary. In all cases, the advance amount shall not exceed Rs. 2,50,000. Requests for amounts higher than these limits require explicit approval from the HR Director and the Chief Financial Officer and will be considered only in exceptional documented circumstances such as major medical emergencies or natural disasters.

**6. Repayment Terms**

Salary advances are recovered through equal monthly deductions from the employee's net salary, beginning from the month immediately following disbursement. The repayment schedule is as follows. Advances up to Rs. 50,000 must be repaid over a maximum of 3 months. Advances from Rs. 50,001 to Rs. 1,00,000 must be repaid over a maximum of 6 months. Advances from Rs. 1,00,001 to Rs. 2,50,000 must be repaid over a maximum of 10 months. The employee may choose to repay sooner or make lump-sum partial payments, which will be adjusted in the repayment schedule. Monthly deductions will not exceed 30 percent of the employee's net take-home salary. If this limit is breached, the repayment tenure will be extended accordingly, subject to HR approval.

**7. Emergency Advance**

In cases of urgent and unforeseen financial need, an employee may apply for an emergency advance of up to Rs. 25,000 irrespective of their band, provided they have not had an emergency advance in the previous 12 months. The emergency advance is processed within 2 working days of approval. It is recovered in a single deduction in the following month's payroll. Employees who receive an emergency advance remain ineligible for a regular salary advance until the emergency advance is fully repaid and a further 3 months have elapsed.

**8. Process for Requesting a Salary Advance**

Step 1 is the employee application. The employee must submit a Salary Advance Request Form through the HRMS portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com under the Finance section. The form must include the requested advance amount, the reason for the advance, and the preferred repayment schedule. Supporting documentation such as hospital bills, legal notices, or any other relevant evidence may be attached to support the application but is not mandatory for amounts below Rs. 1,00,000. Step 2 is manager acknowledgment. The request is automatically routed to the reporting manager for acknowledgment within 2 working days. The manager does not approve or reject the advance but acknowledges awareness of the request. Step 3 is HR review. The HR Business Partner reviews the request to confirm the employee's eligibility and current financial commitments including any outstanding loans from the company. HR approves the advance amount and repayment schedule within 3 working days of manager acknowledgment. Step 4 is payroll processing. Upon HR approval, the payroll team processes the advance disbursement in the next available payroll cycle. If the request is received before the 10th of the month, the disbursement is processed in the same month's payroll. Requests received after the 10th are disbursed in the following month's payroll. Step 5 is documentation. HR issues a Salary Advance Agreement confirming the disbursed amount and the repayment schedule. The employee must sign this document through HRMS within 5 working days of receiving the advance. Failure to sign within this window may result in the advance being recovered in full in the next payroll cycle.

**9. Treatment on Resignation or Termination**

In the event of an employee's resignation, the outstanding balance of any salary advance will be deducted in full from the Full and Final Settlement payout. In the event of termination, the outstanding advance balance will be recovered from the final settlement and any other dues payable. If the outstanding advance exceeds the final settlement amount, AkhilaPrime Solutions reserves the right to recover the remaining balance through other legal means. Employees on notice period are not eligible to apply for new salary advances or emergency advances.

**10. Confidentiality**

All salary advance requests and approvals are treated as strictly confidential. The details of an employee's salary advance must not be disclosed by HR or managers to other employees. Access to advance records is restricted to the employee, HR Business Partner, HR Director, payroll team, and Chief Financial Officer.

**11. Taxation**

Salary advances are not treated as income and are therefore not subject to TDS at the time of disbursement. However, if any portion of the advance is waived or written off by the company, the waived amount may be treated as a perquisite under the Income Tax Act and will be taxed accordingly. Employees are encouraged to consult a tax advisor for guidance specific to their situation.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for accurately stating the reason for the advance, signing the advance agreement, and ensuring timely repayment. The reporting manager is responsible for acknowledging the application within the stipulated time. The HR Business Partner is responsible for reviewing eligibility, approving the advance, and maintaining advance records. The payroll team is responsible for disbursement and monthly recovery deductions. The HR Director is responsible for approving exceptional advance requests above standard limits.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR and the Finance team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
