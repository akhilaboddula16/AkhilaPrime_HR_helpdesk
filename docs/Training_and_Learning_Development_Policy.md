# Training and Learning Development Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy outlines AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd.'s commitment to continuous employee learning, skill development, and professional growth. AkhilaPrime Solutions believes that investing in employees' capabilities is fundamental to the company's long-term competitiveness and to individual career progression. This policy defines the types of learning support available, the eligibility criteria, the process for requesting and approving learning interventions, the financial support provided, and the obligations of employees who receive company-sponsored training.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Probationary employees are eligible for mandatory onboarding training and compliance training but are not eligible for external certification sponsorship until confirmation of employment. Contractual staff and consultants are not covered under this policy.

**3. Learning and Development Philosophy**

AkhilaPrime Solutions follows a 70-20-10 learning philosophy. Seventy percent of learning is expected to occur through on-the-job experience including project assignments, stretch tasks, and cross-functional collaboration. Twenty percent of learning is expected to occur through social learning, including mentoring, peer feedback, internal knowledge-sharing sessions, and manager coaching. Ten percent of learning is expected to occur through formal structured learning such as courses, certifications, and workshops. HR and managers are expected to encourage and facilitate all three modes of learning actively.

**4. Types of Learning Interventions**

**4.1. Mandatory Training**

All employees at AkhilaPrime Solutions are required to complete the following mandatory training programmes, which are provided at no cost to the employee and are conducted during working hours. POSH Awareness Training must be completed within the first 30 days of joining and must be refreshed annually. Code of Conduct and Ethics Training must be completed within the first 30 days of joining. Data Security and IT Security Training must be completed within the first 30 days of joining and must be refreshed annually. Any other compliance training mandated by HR or the Legal team with adequate notice. Failure to complete mandatory training by the stipulated deadline is treated as a disciplinary matter as per the Disciplinary Policy.

**4.2. Role-Specific Technical Training**

Managers are responsible for identifying role-specific training needs for their team members at the start of each appraisal year and incorporating these into each employee's Individual Development Plan (IDP). Role-specific training may include internal workshops, vendor-led training sessions, tool-specific certification programmes, or external instructor-led courses. The cost of approved role-specific training is fully borne by AkhilaPrime Solutions subject to the budget limits defined in Section 6.

**4.3. External Certifications**

AkhilaPrime Solutions sponsors external professional certifications relevant to an employee's current role or career path within the company. Eligible certifications include cloud and DevOps certifications such as AWS, Azure, and GCP certifications, programming and software engineering certifications, project management certifications such as PMP and PRINCE2, data and AI certifications, and any other certification deemed strategically relevant by the department head and HR. Employees wishing to pursue a certification must submit a Certification Sponsorship Request through the HRMS Learning Module before registering for the exam. Approvals are required from the reporting manager and HR Business Partner before any fees are committed.

**4.4. Higher Education Assistance**

Employees who wish to pursue part-time or distance learning degree programmes or executive education courses at recognised universities may apply for partial financial assistance. AkhilaPrime Solutions provides education assistance of up to Rs. 50,000 per year for approved higher education programmes. This assistance is provided as a reimbursement upon submission of fee receipts and subject to the employee maintaining satisfactory performance during the programme. Approval is at the discretion of the HR Director and applies only to programmes that are directly relevant to the employee's role or career growth within the company.

**4.5. Internal Learning Sessions and Communities**

AkhilaPrime Solutions encourages a culture of peer learning through the following internal initiatives. Tech Talks are monthly knowledge-sharing sessions hosted by engineers and architects to share learnings from projects, new tools, or industry trends. Learning Circles are voluntary small-group study sessions organised around topics such as machine learning, distributed systems, or product management. Internal Mentoring Programme pairs senior employees with junior employees for 6-month mentoring engagements. Employees are encouraged to contribute to these initiatives as both participants and facilitators.

**5. Individual Development Plan**

Every confirmed employee must create an Individual Development Plan in the HRMS Learning Module at the beginning of each appraisal cycle in April, in consultation with their reporting manager. The IDP must identify at least 2 learning goals for the year, each linked to a specific skill gap or career objective. It must specify the planned learning interventions such as courses, certifications, and mentoring that will address these goals. It must include a timeline and measurable success criteria for each goal. The IDP is reviewed as part of the mid-year appraisal check-in and the annual performance review. Completion of IDP goals contributes to the employee's overall performance rating.

**6. Training Budget and Financial Limits**

AkhilaPrime Solutions allocates an annual Learning and Development budget as follows. Band 1 to Band 3 employees are eligible for up to Rs. 20,000 per year in sponsored external training and certification costs. Band 4 to Band 5 employees are eligible for up to Rs. 40,000 per year. Band 6 and above employees are eligible for up to Rs. 75,000 per year. Unused budget does not carry forward to the next year. In addition to individual limits, the department may have a pooled L&D budget managed by the department head, which may be deployed for group training programmes, team workshops, or conference sponsorships. Travel and accommodation costs associated with external training are reimbursed under the Travel and Expense Policy.

**7. Process for Requesting External Training or Certification**

Step 1 is the employee submitting a Training Sponsorship Request through the HRMS Learning Module, specifying the training name, provider, cost, dates, and business relevance. Step 2 is the reporting manager reviewing and approving the request within 5 working days. The manager confirms that the training is relevant to the employee's role, that the employee's availability during the training period will not significantly impact ongoing projects, and that sufficient L&D budget is available. Step 3 is the HR Business Partner reviewing and providing final approval, confirming budget availability and policy compliance. Step 4 is registration. The employee may register and pay for the training only after receiving HR approval. Reimbursements for unapproved expenses will not be processed. Step 5 is the reimbursement submission. The employee submits fee receipts through the HRMS expense module within 30 days of completing the training to claim reimbursement.

**8. Post-Training Obligations**

An employee who receives external training sponsorship of Rs. 10,000 or above has the following obligations. The employee must submit a Post-Training Report through HRMS within 10 working days of completing the training, summarising key learnings and how they will apply them to their work. The employee must share learnings with their team through an internal presentation, Tech Talk, or written summary post within 30 days. If the employee leaves AkhilaPrime Solutions voluntarily within the following periods after receiving sponsored training, a proportionate cost recovery will apply. If the employee leaves within 6 months of completing training costing Rs. 10,000 or above, 100 percent of the training cost will be recovered from the Full and Final Settlement. If the employee leaves between 6 and 12 months, 50 percent will be recovered. Exits due to company-initiated termination, medical grounds, or family relocation are exempt from cost recovery.

**9. Conference and Event Participation**

Employees may apply to attend industry conferences, seminars, or meetups relevant to their domain. Attendance at external events is subject to manager and HR approval and must be tied to a specific learning objective. Conference registration fees, travel, and accommodation are reimbursed under the Travel and Expense Policy. Employees who present a paper, demo, or talk at a conference as a representative of AkhilaPrime Solutions must obtain prior approval from their department head and the Marketing team, and must ensure that no confidential company or client information is disclosed.

**10. Training Records**

All completed training activities including mandatory training, certifications, external courses, and internal sessions must be logged in the HRMS Learning Module by the employee within 10 working days of completion. Certificates must be uploaded to the HRMS Learning Module for the employee's record. These records are considered during promotions, role change evaluations, and performance assessments.

**11. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for proactively identifying skill gaps, creating and pursuing their IDP, completing mandatory training on time, and logging all completed training in HRMS. The reporting manager is responsible for identifying team-level skill gaps, supporting employees in creating meaningful IDPs, approving training requests promptly, and discussing L&D progress during performance conversations. The HR Business Partner is responsible for communicating the L&D budget, administering the policy, approving training requests, and reporting on L&D utilisation to HR leadership. The HR Director is responsible for setting the overall L&D budget, approving higher education assistance, and reviewing the effectiveness of the L&D programme annually.

**12. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
