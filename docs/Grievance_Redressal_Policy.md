# Grievance Redressal Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. is committed to maintaining a fair, transparent, and respectful work environment for all employees. This Grievance Redressal Policy provides a structured mechanism through which employees can raise concerns, complaints, or grievances related to their employment, working conditions, interpersonal conflicts, or perceived violations of company policies. The objective is to ensure that all grievances are addressed promptly, impartially, and confidentially without fear of retaliation.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. including permanent, probationary, and contractual staff across all office locations in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai. Grievances related to sexual harassment are governed separately under the Prevention of Sexual Harassment Policy (POSH) and the Internal Complaints Committee.

**3. Definition of Grievance**

A grievance is any genuine concern, dissatisfaction, or complaint raised by an employee in connection with their employment at AkhilaPrime Solutions. Examples of grievances include but are not limited to: unfair treatment or discrimination based on gender, caste, religion, age, or disability; concerns related to salary, increments, or benefits; disputes with colleagues or managers regarding work allocation or behaviour; dissatisfaction with performance evaluation outcomes; violations of company policy by management or peers; concerns about workplace safety, health, or facilities; and issues related to wrongful denial of leave, promotion, or transfer.

**4. Grievance Redressal Levels**

**4.1. Level 1 — Informal Resolution**

Employees are encouraged to first attempt an informal resolution by discussing the concern directly with their reporting manager. This should be done within 7 working days of the incident or situation causing the grievance. The manager must acknowledge the concern and attempt resolution within 5 working days of the discussion. If the grievance involves the reporting manager directly, the employee should escalate directly to Level 2.

**4.2. Level 2 — HR Business Partner**

If the grievance is not resolved at Level 1 or if the employee is not satisfied with the outcome, the employee must submit a formal written grievance to the HR Business Partner assigned to their business unit. The submission can be made through the HRMS Grievance Module, by email to grievance@akhilaprime.com, or by submitting a signed physical letter to the HR department. The formal grievance must include the name and employee ID of the complainant, the name of the person against whom the grievance is raised if applicable, a detailed description of the issue with dates and evidence if available, and the resolution sought by the complainant. The HR Business Partner must acknowledge receipt of the grievance within 2 working days and complete an initial investigation within 10 working days of receipt. The employee will be kept informed of progress throughout the investigation.

**4.3. Level 3 — HR Director and Department Head**

If the grievance remains unresolved at Level 2 or if the employee is dissatisfied with the HR Business Partner's decision, the employee may escalate the matter to the HR Director and the concerned Department Head within 5 working days of receiving the Level 2 decision. The HR Director will review the case, conduct any additional investigations as necessary, and provide a final internal decision within 15 working days.

**4.4. Level 4 — External Escalation**

If the employee remains unsatisfied after the internal resolution process, they retain the right to approach external statutory bodies including the Labour Commissioner, applicable courts, or relevant tribunals as provided under Indian labour law. AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. will cooperate fully with any statutory or legal proceedings.

**5. Investigation Process**

Upon receiving a formal grievance, the HR Business Partner will assign an investigation officer who is neutral and not a party to the grievance. The investigation may involve individual interviews with the complainant, the respondent, and any relevant witnesses. All parties involved are required to cooperate fully and maintain confidentiality throughout the process. Documentary evidence, email communications, and attendance records may be reviewed. The investigation findings will be documented in a formal report shared with the HR Director. All parties will be informed of the outcome in writing.

**6. Confidentiality**

All grievances and related information will be treated with strict confidentiality. Information will only be shared with parties directly involved in the investigation or those who need to know to facilitate resolution. Breach of confidentiality by any party involved in the process will itself be treated as a disciplinary matter.

**7. Non-Retaliation**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. strictly prohibits any form of retaliation against an employee who has raised a grievance in good faith. Retaliation includes but is not limited to negative performance reviews, denial of promotion, transfer to undesirable roles, social exclusion, or any other adverse employment action taken in response to a grievance. Any employee found to have engaged in retaliatory behaviour will face disciplinary action.

**8. False Grievances**

Employees are expected to raise grievances genuinely and in good faith. Filing a false, malicious, or frivolous grievance with the intent to harm another employee's reputation or career will be treated as misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

**9. Timelines Summary**

Informal resolution at Level 1 must be completed within 5 working days. Formal grievance acknowledgment at Level 2 is within 2 working days and resolution within 10 working days. Level 3 escalation decision is within 15 working days. Total maximum internal resolution timeline is 32 working days from formal submission.

**10. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for raising genuine grievances through the appropriate channel, providing accurate information, and cooperating with the investigation. The reporting manager is responsible for addressing informal grievances fairly and within the stipulated timeline. HR Business Partners are responsible for managing the formal grievance process, conducting investigations, and ensuring timely resolution. The HR Director is responsible for final internal decisions on unresolved grievances and for policy oversight.

**11. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Any amendments will be notified to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon subsequent revisions.
