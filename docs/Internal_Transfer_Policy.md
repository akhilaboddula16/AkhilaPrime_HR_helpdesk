# Internal Transfer Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy outlines the framework for internal job transfers, role changes, and location transfers at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Internal mobility is a core component of AkhilaPrime Solutions' talent strategy. It allows employees to grow their careers within the organisation, reduces external attrition, and enables business units to access skilled talent from within the company. This policy defines the eligibility criteria, types of internal transfers, the process for requesting and approving transfers, and the terms applicable to transferred employees.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. across all bands, departments, and office locations in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai. Probationary employees, contractual staff, and consultants are not eligible to apply for internal transfers.

**3. Types of Internal Transfers**

**3.1. Internal Job Posting Transfer**

An Internal Job Posting (IJP) transfer occurs when an employee applies for and is selected for an open role within a different team or department, typically at the same or higher band. This is the most common form of internal transfer and follows the process defined in Section 5.

**3.2. Manager-Initiated Transfer**

A manager-initiated transfer occurs when a business need requires an employee to be moved to a different team, project, or department, as determined by management. This is not a disciplinary action and is initiated in the interests of business continuity, skill alignment, or team balancing. Manager-initiated transfers are facilitated by HR and the employee's consent is sought where operationally feasible, though the company reserves the right to transfer employees to any of its offices or departments in line with the terms of the employment contract.

**3.3. Location Transfer**

A location transfer involves the movement of an employee from one AkhilaPrime Solutions office to another, for example from Bangalore to Chennai. Location transfers may be employee-requested or company-initiated. The terms of location transfers including relocation assistance are defined in Section 8.

**3.4. Deputation**

A deputation is a temporary transfer of an employee to a different team, project, or client location for a defined period not exceeding 6 months. The employee continues to receive their existing compensation during deputation. At the end of the deputation period, the employee returns to their original role unless a permanent transfer is mutually agreed upon.

**4. Eligibility Criteria**

To be eligible for an Internal Job Posting transfer, the employee must have completed at least 18 months of continuous service in their current role from the date of confirmation. The employee must have received a performance rating of 3 (Meets Expectations) or above in their most recent annual appraisal. The employee must not be on a Performance Improvement Plan or under active disciplinary proceedings at the time of application. The employee must not have completed an internal transfer within the previous 18 months. The employee must notify their current reporting manager and HR at the time of applying for an Internal Job Posting. Applying in secret without informing the current manager is not permitted and may disqualify the application.

**5. Internal Job Posting Process**

Step 1 is the posting of open roles. HR publishes all open roles on the AkhilaPrime Solutions intranet and HRMS job board at least 7 days before opening the position to external candidates. Internal postings are clearly labelled as open for internal applicants and state the minimum eligibility criteria. Step 2 is the employee application. Eligible employees may apply through the HRMS job board by submitting their updated profile and a brief statement of intent. The employee must simultaneously notify their current reporting manager in writing of their intention to apply. Step 3 is the manager acknowledgment. The current reporting manager must acknowledge the application within 3 working days. The manager may not block the application but may flag operational concerns to HR, such as critical ongoing projects that would be impacted by the employee's departure. Step 4 is the selection process. The hiring manager and HR for the target team conduct the selection process, which may include competency discussions, technical assessments, or case-based evaluations. The internal candidate is evaluated on the same criteria as external candidates. Step 5 is the offer and acceptance. If selected, HR issues an internal transfer offer letter specifying the new role, revised band if applicable, new compensation package, and transfer date. The employee has 5 working days to accept or decline. Step 6 is the transition. Upon acceptance, the employee and current manager agree on a transition period of not more than 30 working days, during which the employee handovers ongoing responsibilities, documentation, and context to the team. The transfer is completed after the agreed transition period. Step 7 is the onboarding in the new role. The new manager and HR facilitate a structured onboarding for the transferred employee covering team introduction, goal setting, and role-specific training as needed.

**6. Compensation Upon Transfer**

In the case of a lateral transfer at the same band, the employee's CTC is reviewed by HR and the new department head. A lateral transfer does not guarantee a salary increase but HR and the hiring manager may offer a retention adjustment if the market rate for the new role warrants it. In the case of a transfer involving a band upgrade, the employee's CTC is revised upwards in line with the compensation band for the new role, subject to the company's compensation structure. In the case of a transfer involving a band downgrade, which applies only to manager-initiated restructuring transfers and not to IJP transfers, the employee's CTC may be revised and will be discussed with the employee with at least 30 days advance notice.

**7. Probationary Period in New Role**

All employees who transfer to a new role under the IJP process are subject to a 60-day probationary period in the new role. During this period, the employee and new manager review whether the transition is working effectively. If the employee does not meet the expectations of the new role within 60 days, HR will discuss options including returning to a suitable role or exploring other alternatives. The employee is not eligible to apply for further internal transfers during the 60-day probationary period in the new role.

**8. Location Transfer and Relocation Assistance**

For company-initiated location transfers, AkhilaPrime Solutions provides the following relocation assistance. A lump-sum relocation allowance of Rs. 75,000 for employees at Band 1 to Band 3, Rs. 1,00,000 for Band 4 to Band 5, and Rs. 1,50,000 for Band 6 and above is provided. This allowance is paid as a non-taxable reimbursement against actual relocation expenses such as packers and movers charges, travel costs, and initial accommodation. For employee-requested location transfers, relocation assistance is provided at 50 percent of the above rates and is subject to availability of a vacancy or business need at the requested location. Location transfers in either direction require at least 30 working days notice. If the employee reverses a company-initiated location transfer within 12 months by resigning, the relocation allowance will be recovered on a pro-rated basis from the Full and Final Settlement.

**9. Protections Against Misuse**

Managers may not use the transfer process as a means of penalising employees or creating an uncomfortable environment for employees they wish to remove from the team. Such behaviour constitutes an act of misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy and is also a form of Quiet Firing, which is explicitly prohibited as per the HR Glossary and Company Values. Employees who believe they are being pressured into an involuntary transfer as a form of retaliation or constructive dismissal may raise a grievance under the Grievance Redressal Policy.

**10. Confidentiality**

Internal transfer applications and discussions are confidential between the employee, the current manager, the target hiring manager, and HR. Candidates who are not selected for an internal role will receive feedback from HR and the hiring manager within 5 working days of the final decision.

**11. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for checking eligibility, notifying the current manager, applying through the proper HRMS channel, and completing the transition period diligently. The current reporting manager is responsible for acknowledging the application, facilitating a smooth transition, and providing constructive feedback to HR if there are business concerns. The hiring manager in the target team is responsible for conducting a fair evaluation, providing timely feedback, and facilitating onboarding for the transferred employee. HR is responsible for maintaining the Internal Job Posting board, administering the process, ensuring eligibility compliance, and managing transfer documentation.

**12. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
