# AI Tool Usage Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the acceptable and prohibited uses of Artificial Intelligence tools and services by employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. in the course of their work. AI tools including large language models, code generation assistants, image generation tools, and other AI-powered services have become integral to modern software development and knowledge work. AkhilaPrime Solutions encourages the responsible use of AI tools to improve productivity, quality, and innovation. At the same time, the use of AI tools without appropriate safeguards can expose the company and its clients to data confidentiality risks, intellectual property risks, legal liability, and quality failures. This policy establishes clear guidelines to enable employees to benefit from AI tools while managing these risks.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, and interns of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who use AI tools in the course of their work, whether on company-owned devices or personal devices used for work purposes.

**3. Definition of AI Tools**

For the purposes of this policy, AI tools include any software, application, or service that uses machine learning or artificial intelligence to generate, summarise, translate, classify, or otherwise process text, code, images, audio, or other content. This includes but is not limited to large language models such as ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Copilot, AI-powered code assistants such as GitHub Copilot, Amazon CodeWhisperer, and Cursor, AI image generation tools such as DALL-E, Midjourney, and Stable Diffusion, AI-powered search and research tools, and AI features embedded in productivity software such as Microsoft 365 Copilot and Google Workspace AI.

**4. Approved AI Tools**

AkhilaPrime Solutions maintains an Approved AI Tools List, which is updated quarterly by the IT Security team in consultation with the Legal team. The current approved tools are accessible on the company intranet and HRMS portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com under the IT Policies section. Employees may use any tool on the Approved AI Tools List for work purposes without separate approval. Employees who wish to use an AI tool not currently on the approved list must submit a request to the IT Security team at security@akhilaprime.com with a description of the tool and its intended use. IT Security will evaluate the tool for data privacy and security compliance and respond within 10 working days.

**5. Permitted Uses of AI Tools**

Employees are encouraged to use approved AI tools for the following purposes. Writing assistance includes drafting, editing, and improving professional communications, documentation, and reports. Code generation and review includes using AI assistants to generate boilerplate code, suggest implementations, identify bugs, and review code quality. Research and summarisation includes using AI tools to summarise publicly available information, research market trends, and synthesise knowledge from non-confidential sources. Learning and development includes using AI tools to learn new technologies, understand unfamiliar codebases, and explore best practices. Prototyping and ideation includes using AI tools to brainstorm ideas, generate mock-ups, and accelerate early-stage development.

**6. Prohibited Uses of AI Tools**

The following uses of AI tools are strictly prohibited at AkhilaPrime Solutions regardless of whether the tool is on the approved list.

**6.1. Inputting Confidential or Restricted Data**

Employees must never input confidential or restricted data into any external AI tool. This includes client data, source code from client projects, proprietary algorithms, internal financial data, employee personal data, trade secrets, and any other information classified as Confidential or Restricted under the Data Protection and Confidentiality Policy. Most publicly available AI tools process and potentially store all inputs on external servers. Inputting confidential data into such tools is equivalent to sending it to an unknown third party and constitutes a serious data breach under the Data Protection and Confidentiality Policy.

**6.2. Submitting AI-Generated Content Without Review**

Employees must never submit AI-generated code, documentation, legal text, financial analysis, or client-facing content as their own work without reviewing and validating it. AI tools can produce plausible but factually incorrect, legally problematic, or insecure outputs. The employee who submits the content is responsible for its accuracy and quality regardless of whether it was AI-generated.

**6.3. Using AI to Circumvent Security Controls**

Employees must not use AI tools to attempt to bypass, reverse-engineer, or exploit security systems, access controls, or encryption used by the company or its clients. Such actions constitute a serious security violation and may result in criminal liability under the IT Act.

**6.4. Generating Misleading or Harmful Content**

Employees must not use AI tools to generate content that is defamatory, discriminatory, fraudulent, or harmful to individuals, clients, or the company. This includes generating fake reviews, deepfakes, misinformation, or any content designed to deceive.

**6.5. Intellectual Property Violations**

Employees must not use AI tools to reproduce copyrighted content in a manner that infringes intellectual property rights. AI-generated code may in some cases reproduce open-source code with restrictive licences. Employees using AI for code generation must verify that the output does not introduce incompatible open-source licence obligations into proprietary codebases.

**7. Client Engagements**

When working on client projects, the use of AI tools is subject to additional restrictions. Employees must check the client's contract and specific project guidelines before using any AI tool on client work. Some clients explicitly prohibit the use of AI tools for their deliverables. If a client contract is silent on AI tool usage, the employee must seek written clarification from the project manager and the Legal team before using AI tools on that engagement. Client data must never be input into any external AI tool under any circumstances, as defined in Section 6.1.

**8. AI-Generated Code in Production**

All AI-generated code that is intended for production deployment must go through the same code review, testing, and security scanning processes as human-written code. Developers are responsible for understanding and being able to explain all code they submit for review, regardless of whether it was AI-assisted. The use of AI tools does not reduce the developer's accountability for the quality, security, and correctness of the code.

**9. Transparency and Disclosure**

Employees are encouraged to be transparent with their team about their use of AI tools, particularly when AI assistance has been significant. Teams should discuss AI tool usage norms openly as part of their ways of working. Employees are not required to disclose AI tool usage to clients unless the client contract specifically requires it, or unless the usage involved inputs that violated Section 6.1.

**10. Personal Use of AI Tools**

Employees may use AI tools for personal purposes on personal devices using personal accounts outside of working hours. However, they must ensure that no company or client data is ever input into personal AI tool accounts, and they must not use AI tools for any purpose that would violate the Employee Code of Conduct Policy.

**11. Consequences of Violation**

Violation of this policy, particularly inputting confidential or client data into unapproved AI tools, is treated as a serious data security breach under the Data Protection and Confidentiality Policy and the Disciplinary Policy. Disciplinary action may range from a formal written warning to termination of employment, depending on the severity and impact of the violation.

**12. Training and Awareness**

The IT Security team will provide periodic training and guidance on the responsible use of AI tools. The Approved AI Tools List and associated guidance will be updated quarterly. Employees are encouraged to share useful AI tool discoveries with the team through the internal Tech Talk programme described in the Training and Learning Development Policy.

**13. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for using only approved AI tools, never inputting confidential data into external AI systems, reviewing all AI-generated outputs before use, and reporting any suspected data exposure. The IT Security team is responsible for maintaining the Approved AI Tools List, evaluating new tool requests, and providing training on AI tool security. The Legal team is responsible for advising on intellectual property, client contract obligations, and regulatory compliance related to AI tool usage. The HR Director is responsible for enforcing this policy and taking disciplinary action in cases of violation.

**14. Policy Review**

Given the rapidly evolving nature of AI technology, this policy is reviewed every 6 months by the IT Security team and Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
