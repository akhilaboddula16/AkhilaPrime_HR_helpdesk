# Employee Promotion Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the framework for employee promotions at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. A promotion is the advancement of an employee to a higher band, accompanied by greater responsibilities, a revised job title, and an enhanced compensation package. AkhilaPrime Solutions is committed to a merit-based, transparent, and fair promotion process that rewards consistent high performance, demonstrated competency at the next level, and alignment with the company's values. This policy ensures that promotion decisions are made consistently across teams and are clearly communicated to employees so they understand what is required to grow within the organisation.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. across all bands and departments. Probationary employees are not eligible for promotion until they have been confirmed. Promotions within the same band, such as a title change without a band change, are managed by the department head and HR as a reclassification and do not follow the full promotion process defined in this policy.

**3. Types of Promotions**

**3.1. Cycle-Based Promotion**

A cycle-based promotion occurs as part of the annual performance appraisal cycle conducted every April. This is the primary promotion mechanism at AkhilaPrime Solutions. Eligible employees are evaluated, nominated, and approved through the structured process defined in Section 5.

**3.2. Off-Cycle Promotion**

An off-cycle promotion may be initiated outside the annual appraisal cycle in exceptional circumstances such as a critical role vacancy being filled internally, a significant expansion of an employee's responsibilities, or a retention risk for a high performer receiving a competing offer. Off-cycle promotions require approval from the HR Director and the department head and follow the same criteria as cycle-based promotions. They are limited to no more than one off-cycle promotion per employee per financial year.

**4. Eligibility Criteria**

To be considered for a promotion, an employee must meet all of the following minimum eligibility criteria. The employee must have completed at least 18 months of continuous service in their current band from the date of confirmation or the date of their last promotion, whichever is later. The employee must have received a performance rating of 4 (Exceeds Expectations) or above in their most recent annual appraisal. The employee must not be on a Performance Improvement Plan or under active disciplinary proceedings at the time of the promotion cycle. The employee must have demonstrated key competencies at the next band level, as assessed by the reporting manager during the promotion evaluation process.

**5. Promotion Process**

Step 1 is the manager nomination. During the annual appraisal cycle, reporting managers identify eligible employees for promotion and submit formal promotion nominations through the HRMS Performance Module by the nomination deadline communicated by HR, typically in February each year. The nomination must include a written justification covering the employee's performance over the review period, evidence of next-level competency demonstration, business impact delivered, and peer and stakeholder feedback. Step 2 is the calibration committee review. HR convenes a Promotion Calibration Committee comprising HR Business Partners and senior department heads to review all nominations across the organisation. The committee ensures consistency in promotion standards across teams and prevents bias or favouritism in individual manager decisions. The committee meets in March each year. Step 3 is the HR Director approval. The calibration committee's recommendations are submitted to the HR Director for final approval. The HR Director may approve, defer, or decline nominations based on the calibration committee's review and budget considerations. Step 4 is the communication. Approved promotions are communicated to employees by their reporting manager, followed by a formal Promotion Letter issued by HR. Promotions take effect from April 1st of the appraisal year. Employees whose nominations were not approved will receive constructive feedback from their manager and HR on the areas they need to develop for future consideration.

**6. Promotion Criteria by Band**

**6.1. Band 1 to Band 2**

Promotion from Band 1 to Band 2 requires consistent delivery of assigned tasks with minimal supervision, demonstrated initiative in improving team processes, positive peer and manager feedback, and completion of at least one significant project contribution.

**6.2. Band 2 to Band 3**

Promotion to Band 3 requires independent ownership of a defined scope of work, demonstrated technical or functional depth, mentoring of Band 1 colleagues, and evidence of cross-team collaboration.

**6.3. Band 3 to Band 4**

Promotion to Band 4 is a significant step and represents the transition from individual contributor to a senior contributor or emerging lead. It requires a track record of delivering complex work independently, demonstrable influence on team direction, technical or functional thought leadership, and consistent above-expectations performance over at least two consecutive appraisal cycles.

**6.4. Band 4 to Band 5**

Promotion to Band 5 requires demonstrated people leadership or technical leadership at scale, ownership of multi-quarter outcomes, strong stakeholder management, and a measurable positive impact on team productivity or product quality.

**6.5. Band 5 and above**

Promotions at Band 5 and above are strategic in nature and are evaluated by the senior leadership team in consultation with the HR Director. In addition to performance, these promotions consider business need, succession planning, and organisational readiness.

**7. Compensation Upon Promotion**

A promotion is accompanied by a salary revision to bring the employee's CTC in line with the compensation band for their new level. The minimum salary increase upon promotion is 15 percent of the employee's current CTC. The actual increase may be higher depending on the compensation band range and the employee's position within the band. The revised compensation takes effect from the date the promotion is effective, typically April 1st. Employees who receive a promotion in the same cycle as an annual increment will receive the promotion increase in addition to the increment, applied separately.

**8. Promotion vs Performance Increment**

A promotion is distinct from an annual performance increment. An annual increment rewards sustained performance at the current level. A promotion rewards demonstrated readiness to operate at a higher level of responsibility and complexity. An employee may receive one, both, or neither in a given cycle depending on their performance and eligibility.

**9. Feedback for Non-Promoted Employees**

Employees who were nominated but not promoted, or who were eligible but not nominated, are entitled to a structured feedback conversation with their reporting manager within 30 days of the promotion cycle outcomes being announced. This conversation must clearly explain the gap between the employee's current performance and what is required for promotion, and must result in a documented development plan with specific milestones to be tracked over the next 12 months.

**10. Promotion Freeze**

In periods of organisational restructuring, financial difficulty, or company-wide promotion freeze announced by the HR Director, the cycle-based promotion process may be paused or modified. Employees will be informed with at least 30 days notice if a promotion freeze is being implemented. Promotion freeze periods do not affect performance appraisals or annual increments unless separately announced.

**11. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for consistently delivering high-quality work, demonstrating next-level competencies, seeking feedback proactively, and discussing career growth with their manager. The reporting manager is responsible for identifying eligible employees, submitting well-documented nominations, providing feedback to non-promoted employees, and creating development plans. The Promotion Calibration Committee is responsible for ensuring consistency and fairness across all nominations. The HR Director is responsible for approving final promotions, managing the promotion budget, and overseeing the process.

**12. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
