# Performance Review and Appraisal Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the performance review framework, appraisal cycle, rating methodology, increment structure, and promotion criteria at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. The objective of this policy is to ensure a fair, transparent, and consistent evaluation of employee performance aligned with individual goals, team objectives, and organisational strategy. The performance management system at AkhilaPrime Solutions is designed to recognise and reward high performers, support average performers in improving, and address underperformance constructively.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Employees who have completed less than 3 months of service in the appraisal cycle will not receive a rating or increment for that cycle but will be eligible from the following cycle. Probationary employees undergo a separate probation review and are not covered under this policy until they are confirmed.

**3. Appraisal Cycle**

The annual appraisal cycle at AkhilaPrime Solutions runs from 1st April to 31st March each year, aligned with the Indian financial year. Performance reviews are conducted once a year in the month of March with outcomes effective from 1st April. Mid-year check-ins are conducted in October and serve as an informal progress review with no rating or monetary outcome. The timeline for the annual appraisal process is as follows: self-assessment by employees opens on 1st March and must be submitted by 10th March; manager evaluations are completed between 11th March and 20th March; moderation and calibration by HR and leadership are conducted between 21st and 25th March; final ratings and increment letters are issued by 31st March.

**4. Goal Setting**

At the beginning of each appraisal cycle on 1st April, all employees must set their goals in the HRMS performance module in consultation with their reporting manager. Goals must follow the SMART framework: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Each employee must have a minimum of 4 and a maximum of 8 goals covering both technical or functional deliverables and behavioural competencies. Goals must be finalised and approved by the reporting manager by 30th April. Goals may be revised mid-year during the October check-in with mutual agreement between the employee and manager.

**5. Performance Rating Scale**

AkhilaPrime Solutions uses a 5-point rating scale for annual performance evaluations.

Rating 5 is Outstanding and is awarded to employees who have significantly exceeded all goals and demonstrated exceptional contribution to the team and organisation. Expected distribution is approximately 10 percent of the workforce.

Rating 4 is Exceeds Expectations and is awarded to employees who have exceeded most goals and consistently delivered above the expected standard. Expected distribution is approximately 20 percent of the workforce.

Rating 3 is Meets Expectations and is awarded to employees who have met all their goals and delivered as per the expected standard. Expected distribution is approximately 50 percent of the workforce.

Rating 2 is Partially Meets Expectations and is awarded to employees who have met some but not all goals and require improvement in specific areas. Expected distribution is approximately 15 percent of the workforce.

Rating 1 is Does Not Meet Expectations and is awarded to employees who have failed to meet most goals and whose performance is significantly below expectations. Expected distribution is approximately 5 percent of the workforce.

**6. Increment Structure**

Annual salary increments are awarded based on performance ratings and are effective from 1st April. Employees rated 5 (Outstanding) receive an increment in the range of 20 to 25 percent of current CTC. Employees rated 4 (Exceeds Expectations) receive an increment in the range of 14 to 19 percent. Employees rated 3 (Meets Expectations) receive an increment in the range of 8 to 13 percent. Employees rated 2 (Partially Meets Expectations) receive an increment in the range of 3 to 7 percent. Employees rated 1 (Does Not Meet Expectations) receive no increment and are placed on a Performance Improvement Plan.

**7. Performance Bonus**

In addition to the annual increment, a performance-linked bonus is paid annually in April based on the overall company financial performance and individual rating. The bonus structure is as follows: Rating 5 receives 20 percent of annual CTC as bonus; Rating 4 receives 15 percent; Rating 3 receives 10 percent; Rating 2 receives 5 percent; Rating 1 receives no bonus. The bonus is subject to the company meeting its annual revenue targets. HR will communicate the bonus payout percentage adjustment in April each year based on company performance.

**8. Mid-Year Check-In**

A mandatory mid-year check-in is conducted in October between the employee and the reporting manager. The purpose of this meeting is to review progress against goals set in April, identify any support or resources needed, discuss career development aspirations, and recalibrate goals if business priorities have shifted. The mid-year check-in does not result in any rating or salary change but is a required activity and must be documented in the HRMS.

**9. Probation Review**

Employees on probation undergo a formal review at the end of their 6-month probation period. The review is conducted by the reporting manager and HR Business Partner. The probation review assesses onboarding performance, skill proficiency, attitude, and cultural fit. Outcomes of the probation review are: confirmation of employment, extension of probation by up to 3 months, or termination of employment. Probation review outcomes are communicated in writing within 10 working days of the review meeting.

**10. Performance Improvement Plan**

Employees who receive a Rating 1 or who show consistent underperformance identified outside the formal cycle will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan. The PIP defines specific improvement targets, support mechanisms, and a timeline of 60 to 90 days. Weekly check-ins between the employee and manager are mandatory during the PIP period. HR Business Partner will monitor progress throughout. Successful completion of a PIP results in the employee continuing employment with an appropriate performance rating. Failure to meet PIP targets may result in role change, demotion, or termination.

**11. Promotion Policy**

Promotions at AkhilaPrime Solutions are merit-based and are reviewed annually during the appraisal cycle. To be eligible for promotion, an employee must have received a Rating 4 or 5 in the current appraisal cycle, have a minimum tenure of 18 months in the current role, have demonstrated competencies required for the next level, and have a vacancy or business need at the higher level. Promotions are subject to budget availability and approval by the department head and HR Director. Promotion decisions are communicated in the April increment letters.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

Employees are responsible for setting SMART goals, completing self-assessments honestly and on time, and actively participating in check-ins. Reporting managers are responsible for providing timely and objective evaluations, giving constructive feedback, and supporting employee development. HR is responsible for managing the appraisal process, conducting calibration sessions, maintaining records, and ensuring fairness and consistency.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR in alignment with business strategy and compensation benchmarks.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any revision.
