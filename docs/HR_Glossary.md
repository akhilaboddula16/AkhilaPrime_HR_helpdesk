# HR Terms and Glossary

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This glossary defines key Human Resources terms, concepts, and abbreviations used across all HR policies, communications, and processes at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Employees and managers are encouraged to refer to this document when they encounter unfamiliar HR terminology in policies, emails, or appraisal documents. This glossary is maintained and updated annually by the HR team.

**2. Compensation and Payroll Terms**

CTC (Cost to Company) refers to the total compensation an employer spends on an employee, including basic salary, allowances, bonuses, employer PF contribution, gratuity provisioning, and all other perquisites. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, CTC is the standard measure used in offer letters and annual increment communications.

Basic Salary is the fixed core component of an employee's monthly pay, constituting 40 percent of CTC at AkhilaPrime Solutions. It forms the base for calculating PF, gratuity, and leave encashment.

HRA (House Rent Allowance) is a salary component paid to employees to cover rental housing expenses. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, HRA is 20 percent of CTC and is eligible for tax exemption under Section 10(13A) of the Income Tax Act subject to conditions.

Variable Pay is the performance-linked component of compensation paid annually based on individual ratings and company financial performance. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, variable pay constitutes 15 percent of CTC.

Gross Salary is the total monthly salary before deductions including PF, professional tax, and TDS.

Net Salary or Take-Home Salary is the amount credited to the employee's account after all statutory and voluntary deductions.

Retroactive Pay is compensation paid to an employee for work performed in a previous period, typically resulting from a delayed salary revision. AkhilaPrime Solutions processes retroactive pay adjustments within 30 days of the effective revision date.

Payroll Deduction is the amount withheld from an employee's salary for statutory purposes such as EPF contribution, professional tax, and income tax deducted at source.

Overtime refers to work performed beyond standard working hours. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, software employees are not entitled to overtime pay but are eligible for Compensatory Off as per the Attendance Policy.

Wage is the hourly monetary compensation for work performed, applicable to contractual or hourly workers. Full-time employees at AkhilaPrime Solutions receive fixed monthly salaries.

**3. Leave and Attendance Terms**

Earned Leave (EL) also called Annual Leave is paid time off accrued based on service. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, all confirmed employees receive 18 EL days per calendar year.

Sick Leave (SL) is paid leave provided for personal illness or medical appointments. AkhilaPrime Solutions provides 12 sick leave days per year.

Casual Leave (CL) is leave for urgent personal matters or emergencies. AkhilaPrime Solutions provides 6 casual leave days per year.

Loss of Pay (LOP) refers to the deduction of salary for days an employee is absent beyond their approved leave balance.

Floating Holidays are flexible paid time off days that employees can use at their discretion for personal observances or preferences, separate from the standard public holiday list.

Garden Leave is a period during which an employee who has resigned is required to stay away from the workplace while still being paid. It is used to protect business interests during the notice period. AkhilaPrime Solutions may invoke garden leave for senior employees in sensitive roles.

Absenteeism refers to the frequent or prolonged absence of employees without valid reasons. Chronic absenteeism at AkhilaPrime Solutions is addressed under the Attendance and Disciplinary Policy.

Compensatory Off (Comp Off) is a day off granted to an employee in lieu of working on a weekend or public holiday as directed by a manager. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, comp off must be availed within 30 days of accrual.

**4. Performance Management Terms**

Performance Management Cycle is the continuous process of setting goals, monitoring progress, providing feedback, and evaluating performance. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, the formal cycle runs annually from April to March.

KPI (Key Performance Indicator) is a quantifiable metric used to evaluate individual, team, or organisational performance against defined targets.

OKR (Objectives and Key Results) is a goal-setting framework defining specific objectives and measurable outcomes. AkhilaPrime Solutions uses SMART goals within the annual appraisal cycle.

SMART Goals are goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. All AkhilaPrime Solutions employees set SMART goals at the beginning of each appraisal cycle in April.

360-Degree Feedback is a feedback process collecting input from multiple sources including managers, peers, direct reports, and self-assessment. AkhilaPrime Solutions incorporates 360-degree elements in senior-level appraisals.

PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) is a formal, time-bound plan created for employees whose performance is below expectations, defining improvement targets, timelines, and support mechanisms. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, PIPs run for 60 to 90 days.

eNPS (Employee Net Promoter Score) is a metric measuring employee satisfaction by gauging how likely employees are to recommend the company as a great place to work.

Pulse Surveys are short, frequent surveys to gather real-time employee feedback. AkhilaPrime Solutions conducts quarterly pulse surveys through the HRMS portal.

**5. Recruitment and Onboarding Terms**

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is software that manages job postings, applications, and candidate tracking. AkhilaPrime Solutions uses an ATS integrated with its career portal at careers.akhilaprime.com.

Talent Acquisition is the strategic process of attracting, identifying, and hiring skilled individuals to meet business needs.

Employer Branding is the process of promoting AkhilaPrime Solutions as a desirable employer to attract and retain top talent.

Preboarding is the process of engaging new hires between acceptance of the offer and the official start date. AkhilaPrime Solutions sends welcome kits, access credentials, and orientation schedules during preboarding.

Employee Onboarding Software refers to the technology used to manage the onboarding experience. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, onboarding is managed through the HRMS portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com.

Background Verification is the process of verifying candidate information including employment history, educational qualifications, identity, and criminal records. All offers at AkhilaPrime Solutions are subject to successful background verification.

Cost Per Hire is the total cost incurred to hire a new employee, including job posting fees, recruiter costs, and interview expenses.

Recruitment Marketing is the strategic use of marketing to attract potential candidates to AkhilaPrime Solutions as an employer of choice.

**6. Employment and Legal Terms**

EEO (Equal Employment Opportunity) is the principle ensuring all individuals have equal chances of employment regardless of gender, caste, religion, age, or disability. AkhilaPrime Solutions is an equal opportunity employer.

Discrimination refers to unfair treatment of individuals based on protected characteristics such as gender, caste, religion, age, or disability. Discrimination is strictly prohibited at AkhilaPrime Solutions under the Code of Conduct Policy.

Hostile Work Environment is a workplace where harassment, discrimination, or intimidating behaviour makes employees uncomfortable. AkhilaPrime Solutions addresses hostile work environment complaints under the POSH Policy and Grievance Redressal Policy.

Workplace Bullying refers to repeated inappropriate behaviour that creates a hostile or humiliating work environment. It is addressed under the Disciplinary Policy at AkhilaPrime Solutions.

Confidentiality refers to the protection of sensitive employee and company information. All AkhilaPrime Solutions employees sign a Non-Disclosure Agreement at joining.

Disciplinary Action refers to corrective measures taken by the company in response to policy violations or misconduct, ranging from verbal warnings to termination.

Grievance is a formal complaint raised by an employee regarding workplace issues. AkhilaPrime Solutions has a structured 4-level Grievance Redressal Policy.

**7. HR Operations Terms**

HRIS (Human Resources Information System) is an integrated software centralising HR data and processes. At AkhilaPrime Solutions, the HRIS is accessible at hrms.akhilaprime.com.

HR Software refers to technology solutions automating HR processes including payroll, leave, performance, and recruitment.

Employee Lifecycle refers to the stages an employee goes through from recruitment, onboarding, development, performance management, and exit.

Employee Engagement refers to the emotional and psychological commitment employees have towards their work and organisation.

People Operations is a strategic approach to HR that focuses on building positive employee experiences. AkhilaPrime Solutions' HR team functions as a People Operations team.

Succession Planning is the process of identifying and developing internal employees for future leadership roles. AkhilaPrime Solutions conducts annual succession planning for Band 5 and above roles.

Employer Value Proposition (EVP) is the unique set of benefits AkhilaPrime Solutions offers to attract and retain talent, including competitive pay, hybrid work, career growth, and health benefits.

Employee Referral Program is AkhilaPrime Solutions' structured programme rewarding employees for referring candidates who are successfully hired and complete their first 6 months of employment.

**8. Separation and Exit Terms**

Attrition is the natural reduction in workforce size through voluntary resignations, retirements, or other forms of separation.

Turnover Rate is the percentage of employees who leave the company in a given period, used to assess workforce stability.

Exit Interview is a structured conversation between departing employees and HR to gather feedback and understand reasons for resignation. AkhilaPrime Solutions conducts exit interviews for all voluntarily separating employees.

Full and Final Settlement (FnF) is the final payroll computation made after an employee's last working day, including outstanding salary, leave encashment, gratuity if applicable, and other dues or deductions.

Gratuity is a statutory benefit paid to employees upon separation after completing a minimum of 5 years of continuous service, calculated under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972.

Garden Leave is described under the Leave terms above and applies in the separation context when an employee is asked to work out the notice period away from the office.

Bumping is a process in which an employee facing a layoff may displace another employee in a lower-level role if they have seniority and meet the role requirements.

Layoff is the temporary or permanent termination of employment for a group of employees due to business reasons such as restructuring or economic conditions.

Downshifting refers to an employee voluntarily moving to a lower-responsibility role or reduced working hours.

**9. Modern HR and Workplace Culture Terms**

Quiet Quitting refers to employees who remain employed but disengage and limit their effort to only the minimum required by their job description, without formally resigning.

Quiet Firing refers to a management approach of gradually making an employee's work conditions uncomfortable to encourage voluntary resignation rather than formally terminating them. This practice is prohibited at AkhilaPrime Solutions and may be raised as a grievance.

The Great Resignation refers to the large-scale trend of employees voluntarily resigning from jobs, particularly prominent globally post-2021, driven by changing work preferences and priorities.

Moonlighting refers to an employee holding a second job or engaging in paid work outside their primary employment. AkhilaPrime Solutions' position on moonlighting is detailed in the Moonlighting and Conflict of Interest Policy.

Hustle Culture refers to a workplace culture celebrating excessive hard work and long hours. AkhilaPrime Solutions does not promote hustle culture and encourages sustainable productivity and work-life balance.

Employee Burnout is a state of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion caused by prolonged job stress. AkhilaPrime Solutions addresses employee burnout through the Employee Assistance Programme and flexible work options.

Telecommuting or Remote Work refers to working from a location outside the company office using technology. AkhilaPrime Solutions' remote work guidelines are defined in the Work From Home Policy.

Digital Nomad refers to a remote worker who works while travelling. AkhilaPrime Solutions' WFH policy does not currently support digital nomad arrangements for more than 30 days without HR Director approval.

Skills Gap refers to the difference between skills required by the organisation and those possessed by employees. AkhilaPrime Solutions addresses skills gaps through the Learning and Development programme.

Labor Hoarding is the practice of retaining more employees than necessary to avoid future rehiring challenges, typically observed during economic downturns.

**10. Benefits and Statutory Terms**

EPF (Employees Provident Fund) is a statutory retirement savings scheme under the EPF Act 1952, to which both employees and AkhilaPrime Solutions contribute 12 percent of basic salary each.

ESOP (Employee Stock Ownership Plan) is a scheme through which AkhilaPrime Solutions grants eligible employees the right to purchase company shares at a predetermined price after a vesting period. Detailed terms are described in the ESOP Policy.

Gratuity is covered under the Separation Terms above and the Resignation and Exit Policy.

NPS (National Pension System) is a voluntary retirement savings scheme. AkhilaPrime Solutions contributes an additional 10 percent of basic salary for employees who opt into corporate NPS after 2 years of service.

Professional Tax is a state-level tax deducted from the employee's monthly salary as per the applicable state tax slab.

TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is income tax deducted monthly from employee salaries by AkhilaPrime Solutions on behalf of the Income Tax Department as per the employee's declared tax regime.
