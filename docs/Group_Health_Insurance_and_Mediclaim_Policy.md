# Group Health Insurance and Mediclaim Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the group health insurance and mediclaim benefits provided by AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. to its employees and their eligible dependents. AkhilaPrime Solutions believes that the health and wellbeing of employees and their families is a fundamental responsibility of the company as an employer. The company provides a comprehensive group mediclaim insurance policy through an empanelled insurance provider to protect employees from the financial burden of medical emergencies, hospitalisation, and critical illness. This document explains the coverage, sum insured, eligibility, enrolment process, claims procedure, and exclusions applicable to the group health insurance scheme.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Probationary employees are covered under the group health insurance from their date of joining. Contractual staff and consultants are not covered under this policy unless specifically stated in their contract.

**3. Insurance Provider and Policy Details**

AkhilaPrime Solutions partners with a leading IRDAI-registered insurance provider for its group mediclaim policy. The current insurance provider details, policy number, and the insurance coordinator's contact information are available on the HRMS portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com under the Benefits section. The policy is renewed annually on April 1st. HR communicates any changes to coverage, sum insured, or terms to all employees at least 30 days before the renewal date.

**4. Coverage and Sum Insured**

**4.1. Employee Coverage**

All covered employees receive a base sum insured of Rs. 5,00,000 per policy year for hospitalisation expenses. This covers the employee as the primary insured member.

**4.2. Dependent Coverage**

Each employee may enrol the following dependents under the group mediclaim policy at no additional cost to the employee. The eligible dependents are the employee's legally married spouse, and dependent children up to the age of 25 years. The sum insured of Rs. 5,00,000 is a floater amount shared across the employee and all enrolled dependents within the same policy year.

**4.3. Parent Coverage**

Employees may optionally enrol their parents or parents-in-law under the group mediclaim policy. Parent coverage is available at a subsidised premium, with AkhilaPrime Solutions bearing 50 percent of the parent premium and the remaining 50 percent deducted from the employee's monthly salary. The sum insured for parents is Rs. 3,00,000 per floater per policy year, separate from the employee and dependent floater. Only one set of parents, either the employee's parents or parents-in-law, may be enrolled in a given policy year.

**4.4. Top-Up Coverage**

Employees who wish to enhance their coverage beyond the base sum insured may opt for a voluntary top-up plan during the annual enrolment window. Top-up plans are available at group-negotiated rates and the premium is fully borne by the employee through a salary deduction. Details of available top-up plans are shared by HR during the annual enrolment window in March each year.

**5. What Is Covered**

The group mediclaim policy covers the following. Inpatient hospitalisation expenses for a minimum continuous period of 24 hours are covered, including room rent, nursing charges, surgeon and anaesthetist fees, operation theatre charges, diagnostic tests, medicines, and blood. Pre-hospitalisation medical expenses incurred up to 30 days before the date of admission for the same illness are covered. Post-hospitalisation medical expenses incurred up to 60 days after discharge for the same illness are covered. Day care procedures that require less than 24 hours of hospitalisation due to advances in medical technology, such as cataract surgery, chemotherapy, and dialysis, are covered. Ambulance charges up to Rs. 2,000 per hospitalisation for emergency transport to the nearest hospital are covered. Maternity expenses including delivery charges, pre-natal and post-natal care are covered up to Rs. 50,000 per delivery for a maximum of two deliveries during the employee's tenure. New-born baby coverage is provided from birth up to 90 days under the mother's sum insured. Covid-19 hospitalisation is covered under standard inpatient terms.

**6. Room Rent Limits**

Room rent is covered as follows based on the employee's band. Employees at Band 1 to Band 3 are entitled to a shared or general ward room, with a room rent limit of Rs. 3,000 per day. Employees at Band 4 to Band 5 are entitled to a single standard room, with a room rent limit of Rs. 5,000 per day. Employees at Band 6 and above are entitled to a single deluxe room, with a room rent limit of Rs. 8,000 per day. If the employee opts for a room above their eligible category, all associated charges including surgery, doctor fees, and diagnostic costs are proportionately reduced in line with the insurance policy terms.

**7. Cashless Hospitalisation**

AkhilaPrime Solutions' insurance provider maintains a network of over 8,000 empanelled hospitals across India where cashless treatment is available. The list of network hospitals is available on the insurance provider's website and on the HRMS Benefits section. For cashless hospitalisation, the employee or their family member must inform the HR team and the insurance provider's helpdesk at least 48 hours before a planned hospitalisation, or within 24 hours of an emergency hospitalisation. The hospital's insurance desk will coordinate the pre-authorisation directly with the insurance provider. The employee is responsible only for non-covered or co-payment expenses at the time of discharge.

**8. Reimbursement Claims**

For treatment at non-network hospitals or when cashless facility was not availed, the employee may file a reimbursement claim within 30 days of discharge. The following documents must be submitted for a reimbursement claim. The documents required are the duly filled claim form available on HRMS, original discharge summary, original bills and receipts, original investigation and diagnostic reports, doctor's prescription for medicines, and any other documents required by the insurance provider. All reimbursement claim documents must be submitted to hr@akhilaprime.com. HR will coordinate with the insurance provider and the claim will typically be processed within 21 working days of submission of complete documents.

**9. Enrolment Process**

**9.1. New Joiners**

New employees are enrolled in the group mediclaim policy from their date of joining. HR initiates the enrolment process as part of the onboarding workflow. The employee must submit dependent details including names, dates of birth, and relationship through the HRMS Benefits section within 15 days of joining. Dependents not enrolled within 30 days of joining can only be added during the annual enrolment window or upon a qualifying life event as described in Section 9.2.

**9.2. Qualifying Life Events**

Outside the annual enrolment window, dependents may be added to the policy within 30 days of a qualifying life event. Qualifying life events include marriage, birth or adoption of a child, and death of a previously enrolled dependent. The employee must submit the relevant supporting document such as a marriage certificate or birth certificate through HRMS within 30 days of the event.

**9.3. Annual Enrolment Window**

The annual enrolment window opens every year in March for changes effective from April 1st. During this window, employees may add or remove dependents, enrol or discontinue parent coverage, and opt for top-up plans. Changes requested outside the annual enrolment window and outside qualifying life events will not be accepted.

**10. What Is Not Covered**

The following are excluded from the group mediclaim policy. Outpatient consultations, diagnostic tests, and medicines not related to a hospitalisation are not covered. Dental treatment unless requiring hospitalisation is not covered. Vision correction including spectacles, contact lenses, and Lasik surgery is not covered. Cosmetic or aesthetic treatments and procedures are not covered. Self-inflicted injuries are not covered. Treatment for substance abuse or addiction is not covered. Experimental or unproven treatments are not covered. Pre-existing conditions are covered after a waiting period of 30 days from the date of first enrolment, except in the case of a claim arising from an accident.

**11. Continuity of Coverage During Leave**

Employees on approved leave including maternity leave, paternity leave, or medical leave continue to be covered under the group mediclaim policy for the duration of their approved leave.

**12. Coverage on Separation**

Group mediclaim coverage ceases on the employee's last working day. Employees who are separating are encouraged to arrange alternative health insurance coverage promptly. HR will provide the employee with a certificate of insurance coverage duration upon request, which may be useful for porting the policy to an individual plan under IRDAI portability rules.

**13. Insurance Helpdesk and Escalations**

For insurance-related queries including claim status, hospital network information, pre-authorisation, and policy details, employees may contact hr@akhilaprime.com. HR will coordinate with the insurance provider's dedicated relationship manager assigned to AkhilaPrime Solutions. For urgent cashless pre-authorisation, employees should call the insurance provider's 24-hour helpline number listed on the HRMS Benefits section and their insurance e-card.

**14. Insurance E-Card**

All enrolled employees and their dependents receive a digital insurance e-card issued by the insurance provider. The e-card is available for download through the HRMS Benefits section and must be presented at the hospital at the time of admission for cashless treatment. Employees must ensure their dependents are aware of the e-card and the cashless hospitalisation process.

**15. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for enrolling dependents on time, keeping HRMS dependent information up to date, carrying the insurance e-card, and submitting claims with complete documentation within the stipulated timelines. HR is responsible for managing the annual policy renewal, coordinating enrolment, maintaining the insurance provider relationship, assisting employees with claims, and communicating policy updates. The Finance team is responsible for processing premium deductions for optional parent coverage and top-up plans through payroll.

**16. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually at the time of insurance renewal in March. Any changes to coverage, sum insured, or premiums will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice before the April 1st renewal.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
