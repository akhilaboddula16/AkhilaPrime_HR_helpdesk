# Probation and Confirmation Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the probationary period framework at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd., including its duration, the expectations placed on probationary employees, the confirmation process, the extension of probation, and the separation process during probation. The probationary period serves as a structured mutual evaluation phase during which AkhilaPrime Solutions assesses whether the new employee meets the performance and conduct expectations of the role, and the employee assesses whether the company and role are the right fit for their career. This policy ensures the probation and confirmation process is transparent, fair, and consistently applied.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all new employees joining AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. in a permanent full-time capacity, regardless of band, department, or office location. Employees joining through internal transfers from another department or location are not subject to a fresh probation but do undergo a 60-day assessment period as defined in the Internal Transfer Policy. Contractual employees, interns, and consultants are not covered under this policy.

**3. Probation Period Duration**

The standard probation period at AkhilaPrime Solutions is 6 months from the date of joining. For roles at Band 1 to Band 3, the probation period is 3 months from the date of joining. For roles at Band 4 to Band 6, the probation period is 6 months from the date of joining. For roles at Band 7 and above, the probation period is 6 months from the date of joining. The probation period begins on the date of joining as documented in the offer letter and in HRMS, and ends on the last day of the applicable period unless extended as described in Section 6.

**4. Expectations During Probation**

During the probation period, the employee is expected to complete all mandatory onboarding training within the first 30 days, as specified in the Employee Onboarding Guide. The employee must familiarise themselves with all relevant company policies including the Code of Conduct, POSH Policy, Data Security guidelines, and IT Asset Policy within the first 30 days. The employee must demonstrate the technical and behavioural competencies required for the role and align with AkhilaPrime Solutions' values of ownership, collaboration, and continuous improvement. The employee must maintain attendance and punctuality as per the Attendance Policy. The employee is expected to actively participate in setting their probation goals in discussion with their reporting manager within the first 2 weeks of joining.

**5. Manager Responsibilities During Probation**

The reporting manager plays a critical role in the success of a probationary employee. Within the first 2 weeks, the manager must schedule a goal-setting discussion and document 3 to 5 measurable probation goals in the HRMS Performance Module. At the 6-week mark, the manager must conduct an informal check-in to provide early feedback on the employee's progress and identify any support needs. At the mid-point of the probation period, typically at the end of month 1.5 for 3-month probation or end of month 3 for 6-month probation, the manager must conduct a formal mid-probation review and log it in HRMS. If the employee's performance or conduct is below expectations at any point, the manager must raise the concern with the HR Business Partner immediately and not wait until the end of the probation period.

**6. Probation Review and Confirmation**

At the end of the probation period, the manager must complete a Probation Review in the HRMS Performance Module at least 15 days before the probation end date. The review must rate the employee on their performance against probation goals, professional conduct and attitude, team integration and collaboration, and technical skill demonstration. Based on the review, one of the following outcomes is recommended.

**6.1. Confirmation**

If the employee has met or exceeded the expected standards across all evaluation criteria, the manager recommends confirmation. HR issues a formal Confirmation Letter to the employee within 5 working days of the probation end date. The confirmation is effective from the date immediately following the end of the probation period. Upon confirmation, the employee becomes eligible for all benefits and entitlements applicable to confirmed employees as described elsewhere in the HR policies.

**6.2. Extension of Probation**

If the employee shows potential but has not yet fully met the expected standards, the manager may recommend an extension of probation for a further period. The maximum extension at AkhilaPrime Solutions is 3 months, and it may be applied only once. HR issues a Probation Extension Letter to the employee, clearly stating the reasons for extension, the duration, and the specific improvement areas the employee must demonstrate during the extended period. The extension is with the employee's written acknowledgment. At the end of the extended probation, the manager must conduct a final review and recommend either confirmation or separation. A second extension is not permitted.

**6.3. Separation During or at the End of Probation**

If the employee has not met the expected standards despite adequate support and feedback, the manager recommends non-confirmation. HR, in consultation with the HR Director, will initiate separation proceedings. Separation during or at the end of probation requires a notice period of 2 weeks from either the company or the employee, unless the separation is due to gross misconduct which may result in summary dismissal. No performance improvement plan is mandatory before separation during probation, though managers are encouraged to provide adequate feedback and support throughout the period. The employee receives their full and final settlement including salary earned, unused leave encashment as applicable, and any statutory dues.

**7. Entitlements During Probation**

Probationary employees at AkhilaPrime Solutions are entitled to the following. Salary and fixed compensation as stated in the offer letter are paid from the date of joining without any probation-linked deduction. Provident Fund contributions are deducted and deposited as per the Employees' Provident Fund Act from the date of joining, regardless of probation status. Medical insurance coverage under the group health insurance scheme begins from the date of joining. Sick Leave of up to 6 days per year is available from the date of joining. Earned Leave does not accrue during probation and begins to accrue from the date of confirmation. Casual Leave of up to 3 days per year is available from the date of joining. Probationary employees are not eligible for the following benefits until confirmation: performance appraisal increment, ESOP grants, salary advance under the Salary Advance Policy, internal transfer applications, and variable pay or bonus eligibility for cycles during which the majority of the period fell within probation.

**8. Background Verification During Probation**

All probationary employees are subject to background verification as per the Background Verification Policy. Confirmation is contingent on a clean BGV result. If an adverse finding is discovered during BGV while the employee is serving their probation, HR will follow the adverse findings process defined in the Background Verification Policy, which may result in termination of employment during probation.

**9. Notice Period During Probation**

During the probation period, the notice period for voluntary resignation by the employee is 2 weeks. The notice period for company-initiated separation during probation is also 2 weeks, except in cases of gross misconduct where summary dismissal without notice applies. This is distinct from the confirmed employee notice period, which is 90 days (3 months) as stated in the offer letter and Resignation and Exit Policy.

**10. Probation in Case of Re-joining**

A former employee who rejoins AkhilaPrime Solutions in a new role is subject to a full probation period as defined in Section 3, regardless of their prior service with the company. Prior service is not counted towards probation completion.

**11. Communication of Outcome**

HR must ensure that the probation outcome, whether confirmation, extension, or separation, is communicated to the employee in writing through a formal letter at least 5 working days before the probation end date wherever possible. Delayed communication of a probation outcome, where no extension or separation letter is issued by the probation end date, does not automatically constitute confirmation. HR will issue a formal communication within the next 5 working days to confirm the status.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for actively engaging in goal-setting, completing mandatory training on time, seeking feedback proactively, and maintaining expected standards of performance and conduct. The reporting manager is responsible for setting clear goals, providing regular feedback, conducting the mid-probation review and final probation review in HRMS, and escalating concerns to HR promptly. The HR Business Partner is responsible for tracking probation end dates, issuing timely confirmation, extension, or separation letters, and supporting managers in managing underperforming probationers. The HR Director is responsible for approving all probation extension and non-confirmation decisions.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
