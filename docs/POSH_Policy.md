# Prevention of Sexual Harassment Policy (POSH)

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. is committed to providing a safe, respectful, and dignified work environment for all employees, irrespective of gender. This policy has been formulated in accordance with the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act 2013 and the associated Rules of 2013. The purpose of this policy is to prevent sexual harassment at the workplace, establish a mechanism for redressal of complaints, and ensure that all employees can work without fear of harassment, intimidation, or retaliation.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. including permanent, probationary, contractual, temporary, and part-time employees, as well as interns, vendors, clients, and visitors who interact with AkhilaPrime Solutions employees in a work context. It covers all workplace locations including the company offices in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai, client sites, work-related travel, virtual work environments, and company-sponsored events or offsites.

**3. Definition of Sexual Harassment**

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome act or behaviour, whether directly or by implication, of a sexual nature. This includes but is not limited to: physical contact or advances of a sexual nature; demand or request for sexual favours; making sexually coloured remarks, jokes, or comments; showing pornographic or sexually explicit material; sending sexually suggestive messages, emails, or images through any medium; unwelcome staring, leering, or whistling; and any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature. Sexual harassment also includes conduct that creates a hostile, humiliating, or intimidating work environment based on a person's gender or sexual orientation. Quid pro quo harassment, where employment benefits such as promotion or increment are linked explicitly or implicitly to sexual favours, is a particularly serious form of sexual harassment.

**4. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)**

In compliance with the POSH Act 2013, AkhilaPrime Solutions has constituted an Internal Complaints Committee at each of its office locations. Each ICC consists of the following members: a Presiding Officer who is a senior female employee, two internal members who are employees committed to the cause of women in the workplace, and one external member who is a person familiar with issues relating to sexual harassment from an NGO or legal background. The ICC membership is reviewed every 3 years. The names and contact details of ICC members at each location are published on the company intranet and are available with HR. The ICC Presiding Officer can be reached at icc@akhilaprime.com.

**5. Filing a Complaint**

An aggrieved employee who experiences or witnesses sexual harassment may file a written complaint with the ICC at their respective office location. The complaint must be submitted within 3 months of the date of the incident or the last incident in a series of incidents. The complaint should include the name and employee ID of the complainant, the name of the respondent, the date, time, and place of the incident, a detailed description of the incident, names of any witnesses, and any supporting evidence such as screenshots, emails, or messages. In cases where the complainant is unable to file a written complaint due to physical or mental incapacity, a complaint may be filed on their behalf by a legal heir, friend, or colleague with the ICC's permission. The ICC will provide a written acknowledgment of the complaint within 7 working days.

**6. Inquiry Process**

Upon receipt of the complaint, the ICC will conduct a time-bound inquiry as follows. The ICC will send a copy of the complaint to the respondent within 7 working days of receipt, requesting a written response within 10 working days. Both parties will be given equal opportunity to present their case and evidence. The inquiry will be conducted with strict confidentiality. The ICC may interview witnesses and examine documentary evidence. The ICC will complete its inquiry and submit a report to the employer within 60 days of receiving the complaint. The report will contain a finding of facts and a recommendation on action to be taken. The employer will act on the ICC recommendation within 60 days of receiving the report.

**7. Interim Relief**

During the pendency of the inquiry, the ICC may recommend interim relief measures to protect the complainant. These may include transferring the complainant or the respondent to a different team or location, granting special leave to the complainant, or restraining the respondent from reporting on or evaluating the complainant's work.

**8. Consequences of Sexual Harassment**

If the ICC finds the allegation proved, appropriate disciplinary action will be taken against the respondent. This may include a written apology, warning or reprimand, withholding of increment or promotion, suspension, demotion, or termination of employment. In severe cases, the matter may be referred to the police for criminal proceedings. The company may also direct the respondent to pay compensation to the complainant as recommended by the ICC.

**9. False Complaints**

If the ICC finds that the complaint was false or malicious and made with the intent to harm the respondent, appropriate action may be taken against the complainant. However, the inability to substantiate a complaint will not be treated as a false complaint.

**10. Confidentiality**

The identity of the complainant, the respondent, and witnesses, as well as the contents of the complaint, inquiry, and report, are strictly confidential and must not be disclosed to any person not authorised by the ICC. Breach of confidentiality is a punishable offence under the POSH Act and will result in disciplinary action.

**11. Non-Retaliation**

AkhilaPrime Solutions strictly prohibits retaliation against any employee who has filed a complaint, provided testimony, or assisted in an ICC inquiry in good faith. Any retaliation will be treated as a separate act of misconduct and will result in disciplinary action.

**12. Awareness and Training**

AkhilaPrime Solutions will conduct mandatory POSH awareness training for all employees at the time of joining and annually thereafter. Training will cover the definition of sexual harassment, the complaint filing process, ICC details, and employee rights and responsibilities. Attendance at POSH training is compulsory and will be tracked through the HRMS learning module.

**13. Annual Report**

The ICC will prepare an annual report at the end of each calendar year and submit it to the employer and the District Officer as required under the POSH Act. The report will include the number of complaints received, disposed of, pending, and the nature of action taken.

**14. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR and the Legal team in alignment with the POSH Act and any amendments thereto.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision. Acknowledgment indicates that the employee has read, understood, and agreed to abide by this policy.
