# Employee Onboarding Guide

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This guide outlines the complete onboarding journey for all new employees joining AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. A structured onboarding experience ensures new employees integrate quickly, feel welcomed, understand their role and responsibilities, and become productive contributors within the first 90 days. This guide covers the preboarding phase, the first day, and the 30-60-90 day onboarding plan.

**2. Scope**

This guide applies to all new full-time permanent employees and probationary employees joining any office of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, or Chennai. For employees joining in a remote or hybrid capacity, specific remote onboarding instructions are provided in Section 8.

**3. Preboarding Phase (Offer Acceptance to Day 0)**

Preboarding begins from the date the candidate accepts the offer letter and ends on the day before their official joining date. The purpose of preboarding is to ensure the new employee feels welcomed and prepared before their first day so that Day 1 is smooth and productive.

Within 2 working days of offer acceptance, HR will send a welcome email containing the confirmed joining date, reporting time, and office location. The email will include the name and contact number of the HR onboarding coordinator and the reporting manager. It will also include a list of documents to be brought on Day 1 such as educational certificates, previous employment documents, Aadhaar card, PAN card, and passport-size photographs. The email will provide a schedule outline for the first day and dress code information. A digital welcome kit containing company brochure, organisation chart, benefits summary, and HRMS login instructions will be attached.

Within 5 working days of offer acceptance, the IT team will provision the new employee's company laptop, create email account, Microsoft Teams ID, and VPN access. The admin team will prepare the workstation, allocate a system, and arrange a welcome kit including notebook, pen, AkhilaPrime Solutions merchandise, and an ID card application form. The HR team will create the employee's HRMS profile, generate the employee ID, and upload the offer letter and employment contract for digital signature.

The reporting manager must prepare a 30-day role-specific induction plan, identify a buddy from the team to support the new joiner, and ensure the team is informed about the new hire's arrival date and role.

**4. Day 1 Checklist**

The HR onboarding coordinator or a designated HR team member must greet the new employee at the office reception and escort them to their workstation. The following activities must be completed on Day 1.

Document collection involves collecting all original documents for verification and returning them within 2 working days. Joining formalities include completing the joining form in HRMS, signing the employment contract, NDA, Code of Conduct acknowledgment, and POSH policy acknowledgment digitally. IT setup involves handing over the laptop, explaining VPN and system access, providing email credentials, and ensuring all required software is installed and functional. ID card processing involves collecting the form and photograph for ID card generation within 5 working days. Office tour includes showing the new employee their workstation, meeting rooms, cafeteria, restrooms, emergency exits, and first aid location.

Introduction to the team must be conducted by the reporting manager including a team lunch or informal meet wherever feasible. HR orientation involves a 2-hour session covering company overview, mission and values, benefits and leave policies, HRMS navigation, and IT and security guidelines. The buddy introduction ensures the assigned buddy connects with the new employee and makes themselves available for the first 30 days.

**5. First 30 Days — Orientation and Learning**

Days 1 to 5 focus on orientation and company overview. The new employee should complete all joining formalities and documentation. They should attend company-wide orientation sessions conducted by HR covering AkhilaPrime Solutions' history, business verticals, culture, and values. They should meet their reporting manager for an in-depth role briefing. They should review all relevant company policies on the HRMS portal. They should set up tools including email, Teams, project management tools like Jira, and development environments as applicable.

Days 6 to 15 focus on team and departmental integration. The employee should meet individually with all team members to understand ongoing projects and responsibilities. They should shadow experienced colleagues to understand workflows and tools. They should attend all team and departmental meetings as an observer. They should begin reading technical documentation, architecture documents, or process guides relevant to their role.

Days 16 to 30 focus on training and initial contribution. The employee should complete all mandatory compliance trainings on the HRMS learning module including POSH, Code of Conduct, Data Security, and IT Security. They should participate in role-specific technical or functional training arranged by the manager. They should begin working on small, well-defined tasks or modules under supervision. They should have a 30-day check-in meeting with HR and the reporting manager to discuss initial experience, clarify expectations, and address any concerns.

The 30-day milestone goal is for the new employee to understand AkhilaPrime Solutions' products, clients, team structure, tools, and policies, and to have successfully delivered at least one defined small task or deliverable.

**6. Days 31 to 60 — Integration and Contribution**

Days 31 to 45 focus on active project involvement. The employee should begin contributing meaningfully to team projects under guidance. They should attend client meetings or internal reviews as a participant rather than just an observer. They should proactively seek feedback from the manager and peers on their work quality and communication. They should set and finalise their SMART goals for the appraisal cycle in the HRMS performance module in consultation with the manager.

Days 46 to 60 focus on skill enhancement and independent work. The employee should identify specific skill gaps based on their role requirements and discuss a development plan with the manager. They should attend any relevant internal or external workshops or certifications recommended by the manager. They should establish a weekly one-on-one cadence with the reporting manager for feedback and guidance. They should have a 60-day check-in with HR to review integration progress and address any concerns.

The 60-day milestone goal is for the employee to be fully integrated into the team, independently handling assigned tasks, and building trusted relationships with colleagues and stakeholders.

**7. Days 61 to 90 — Performance and Independence**

Days 61 to 75 focus on independent delivery and ownership. The employee should own and deliver a full project module or task independently. They should demonstrate clear understanding of the team's objectives and how their work contributes to them. They should proactively flag risks, blockers, or improvement ideas to the manager. They should contribute to team knowledge by sharing documentation, participating in code reviews, or leading internal knowledge-sharing sessions.

Days 76 to 90 focus on the formal probation review period. The employee and manager should review goal progress against the objectives set in Month 1. The manager should complete the Probation Review form on HRMS before Day 85. HR will schedule the formal probation review meeting to be held between Day 85 and Day 90. Outcomes of the probation review will be communicated in writing within 10 working days of the review meeting.

The 90-day milestone goal is for the employee to be fully independent in their role, to have completed all compliance training, to have established positive working relationships, and to be on track for confirmation of employment.

**8. Remote Employee Onboarding**

For employees joining in a remote or hybrid capacity, the following additional steps apply. IT must ship the company laptop and accessories to the employee's home address at least 3 working days before the joining date. The HR onboarding coordinator must schedule all Day 1 activities virtually via Microsoft Teams. The buddy must be particularly proactive in checking in daily for the first 2 weeks via Teams. Virtual team introductions must be arranged by the manager including a video call with the entire team on Day 1. The employee must ensure their home workspace meets the requirements defined in the Work From Home Policy before their start date.

**9. Key Contacts for New Employees**

For HRMS and joining formalities queries, contact onboarding@akhilaprime.com. For IT setup and access issues, contact it-helpdesk@akhilaprime.com. For ESOP and compensation queries, contact hr@akhilaprime.com. For general HR policies and benefits, contact hr@akhilaprime.com. For ID card and access card queries, contact admin@akhilaprime.com.

**10. Roles and Responsibilities**

HR is responsible for sending the offer and welcome communications, conducting orientation, collecting documentation, managing HRMS setup, and conducting 30, 60, and 90-day check-ins. The reporting manager is responsible for preparing the role-specific induction plan, assigning a buddy, ensuring team integration, providing regular feedback, and completing the probation review. IT is responsible for laptop provisioning, access setup, and technical support during onboarding. The buddy is responsible for being available, answering informal questions, and helping the new employee navigate the culture and processes. The new employee is responsible for completing all mandatory training, actively engaging in onboarding activities, asking questions, and working toward the 30-60-90 day milestones.

**11. Policy Review**

This onboarding guide is reviewed annually by HR and updated based on feedback from new joiner surveys and manager inputs.

**Acknowledgment**

All new employees must acknowledge this onboarding guide through the HRMS portal on or before Day 1 of joining.
