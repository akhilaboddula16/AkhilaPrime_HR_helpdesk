# Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Process

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This document defines the Performance Improvement Plan process at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. A Performance Improvement Plan is a formal, structured, and time-bound intervention used when an employee's performance is significantly below the expected standard. The PIP process is designed to give the employee a fair and supported opportunity to meet performance expectations before any further employment action is taken. It reflects AkhilaPrime Solutions' commitment to treating every employee with respect and providing them a genuine chance to succeed.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who receive a Performance Rating of 1 (Does Not Meet Expectations) in the annual appraisal cycle, or who are identified by their reporting manager and HR as consistently underperforming outside the formal appraisal cycle.

**3. When is a PIP Initiated**

A PIP is initiated under the following circumstances. An employee receives a Rating 1 in the annual performance appraisal cycle. A manager formally escalates persistent underperformance to HR with documented evidence of at least 2 prior informal counselling conversations with the employee. An employee fails to meet critical project milestones or deliverables despite warnings and support. An employee is identified as underperforming during the probation review and an extension of probation is not sufficient.

Before initiating a PIP, the reporting manager must have had at least 2 documented conversations with the employee discussing the performance gaps and providing clear guidance on improvement. These conversations must be recorded in the HRMS performance module. If these prior discussions have not been documented, HR will not approve the PIP initiation.

**4. PIP Initiation Process**

Step 1 is the manager's escalation to HR. The reporting manager submits a formal PIP initiation request to the HR Business Partner through HRMS or by email to hrbp@akhilaprime.com. The request must include the employee's name and ID, the specific performance gaps observed with examples and dates, documentation of prior counselling conversations, and the manager's proposed improvement goals. Step 2 is the HR review. The HR Business Partner reviews the request within 3 working days to assess whether a PIP is appropriate and whether prior counselling was documented. HR may request additional information or suggest alternative interventions such as additional training or role reassignment. Step 3 is the PIP document preparation. Upon HR approval, the manager prepares the formal PIP document in consultation with HR using the PIP template available on the HRMS portal. Step 4 is the PIP meeting. The manager and HR Business Partner meet with the employee to present and discuss the PIP. The employee is informed of the specific areas of concern, the improvement goals, the support available, and the consequences of not meeting the PIP targets.

**5. Contents of the PIP Document**

Every PIP document at AkhilaPrime Solutions must contain the following elements. Employee details including name, employee ID, designation, department, and reporting manager. PIP start date and end date with the PIP duration being 60 to 90 days based on the complexity of the performance issues. Specific areas of concern with concrete examples, dates, and measurable evidence of the gap between expected and actual performance. Improvement goals that are SMART, meaning Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound, along with clear success criteria for each goal. Activity goals describing specific actions the employee must take to achieve each improvement goal. Resources and support available such as training programmes, mentoring, additional tools, or peer support. Weekly check-in schedule specifying the day, time, and format of weekly review meetings between the employee and manager during the PIP period. Consequences clearly stating that failure to meet PIP targets within the specified duration may result in further disciplinary action including demotion, role change, or termination of employment.

**6. During the PIP Period**

The manager must conduct weekly one-on-one check-in meetings with the employee throughout the PIP period. Each check-in must be documented in HRMS with a brief progress note and any updates to goals or timelines. The HR Business Partner must review progress at the midpoint of the PIP, which is typically at the 30-day or 45-day mark, to assess whether the employee is on track. The employee is expected to attend all check-ins, engage proactively with the improvement activities, and demonstrate measurable progress toward the defined goals. The employee may raise concerns about the fairness or clarity of the PIP through the HR Business Partner. If the employee raises a formal grievance related to the PIP, it will be handled under the Grievance Redressal Policy but will not automatically pause the PIP.

Employees on PIP are not eligible for the standard WFH arrangement as defined in the Work From Home Policy. They are required to be present in the office on all standard working days unless they have approved leave.

**7. PIP Outcomes**

At the end of the PIP period, the manager and HR Business Partner jointly assess the employee's performance against the improvement goals and determine one of three outcomes.

Outcome 1 is Successful Completion. If the employee has met all or substantially all of the PIP goals, the PIP is formally closed with a written confirmation of successful completion issued by HR. The employee returns to standard performance management processes. A PIP completion note is added to the employee's HR record. The employee is eligible for standard appraisal consideration in the next cycle.

Outcome 2 is Partial Progress. If the employee has made meaningful progress but has not fully met all goals, the manager and HR may decide to extend the PIP by up to 30 additional days with clearly revised targets. Only one extension per PIP is permitted.

Outcome 3 is Unsuccessful Completion. If the employee has not demonstrated adequate improvement by the end of the PIP period or any approved extension, HR will initiate further action. This may include transfer to a different role or team better suited to the employee's skills, demotion to a lower band with a corresponding salary revision, or termination of employment as a last resort. The decision will be made by HR Director in consultation with the department head and communicated in writing to the employee with at least 7 working days notice.

**8. Employee Rights During PIP**

The employee has the right to a clear and written explanation of all performance gaps and improvement goals. The employee has the right to raise concerns about the fairness of the PIP to the HR Business Partner or HR Director. The employee has the right to request additional training or resources to support their improvement. The employee has the right to appeal the PIP initiation decision to the HR Director within 5 working days of receiving the PIP document if they believe it is unjust. The employee may be accompanied by a colleague of their choice (not an external person or legal counsel) to any formal PIP meeting.

**9. Confidentiality**

All PIP proceedings, documents, and discussions are strictly confidential. The existence of an employee's PIP must not be disclosed by the manager or HR to other employees except those directly involved in the process such as the HR Business Partner and department head.

**10. PIP and Annual Appraisal**

An employee who successfully completes a PIP within the appraisal year will be assessed in the annual appraisal cycle and may receive a Performance Rating of 2 (Partially Meets Expectations) as a minimum, with an opportunity to receive a higher rating if performance in the post-PIP period warrants it. Employees who do not complete the PIP successfully will not receive an increment or bonus for that appraisal cycle.

**11. SMART Goals Framework for PIP**

All improvement goals within a PIP must follow the SMART framework. Specific means the goal clearly defines what needs to be achieved, leaving no ambiguity. Measurable means the goal includes a quantifiable metric to determine whether it has been achieved. Achievable means the goal is realistic and attainable within the PIP timeframe with the available support. Relevant means the goal directly addresses the identified performance gap. Time-bound means the goal has a clear deadline within the PIP period.

Example of a SMART PIP goal: By the end of Week 8 of the PIP, the employee will submit all assigned module deliverables to the team lead for review with zero critical defects, measured against the project acceptance criteria defined in the sprint backlog. This is more effective than a vague goal such as improve code quality which provides no measurable or time-bound target.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The reporting manager is responsible for identifying performance gaps, documenting counselling conversations, preparing the PIP document, conducting weekly check-ins, and assessing PIP outcomes. The HR Business Partner is responsible for reviewing and approving PIP initiation, providing guidance on the PIP document, conducting the mid-PIP review, and supporting outcome decisions. The HR Director is responsible for approving termination or demotion outcomes and handling any appeals related to the PIP. The employee is responsible for engaging constructively with the PIP process, attending all check-ins, taking ownership of their improvement, and communicating barriers to progress proactively.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Any amendments will be communicated to all managers and employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All managers must acknowledge familiarity with this process through the HRMS portal. All employees placed on a PIP must sign the PIP document digitally through HRMS acknowledging receipt and understanding of the improvement goals and consequences.
