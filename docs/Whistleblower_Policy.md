# Whistleblower Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy establishes the framework for reporting, investigating, and acting on concerns related to unethical conduct, fraud, misconduct, or violations of law or company policy at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. AkhilaPrime Solutions is committed to maintaining a culture of transparency, integrity, and accountability. Every employee has a responsibility to speak up when they become aware of wrongdoing. This policy ensures that employees can raise concerns in good faith without fear of retaliation, and that all reports are investigated thoroughly, fairly, and confidentially.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and vendors associated with AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. It covers concerns related to any activity occurring within the company, by its employees, or by third parties acting on the company's behalf.

**3. What Can Be Reported**

Employees may raise concerns under this policy about the following categories of wrongdoing. Financial misconduct includes fraud, falsification of financial records, misappropriation of company funds, expense fraud, or bribery and corruption. Legal violations include breaches of applicable Indian laws such as the Companies Act, Income Tax Act, Prevention of Money Laundering Act, IT Act, or any other statute. Policy violations include serious breaches of AkhilaPrime Solutions' internal policies including the Code of Conduct, POSH Policy, Data Protection Policy, or Conflict of Interest Policy. Safety and environment violations include activities that endanger the physical safety of employees or cause environmental harm. Abuse of authority includes misuse of managerial power, coercion, or deliberate suppression of employee rights. Governance failures include actions by senior leadership that undermine the integrity of the company's governance structures, financial reporting, or audit processes. This policy is not intended for reporting routine HR grievances, performance-related complaints, or personal disputes between colleagues. Those matters are addressed under the Grievance Redressal Policy.

**4. How to Report**

AkhilaPrime Solutions provides the following channels for raising a whistleblower concern. The primary channel is the Ethics Helpline, which can be reached by email at ethics@akhilaprime.com. This mailbox is monitored exclusively by the Ethics Committee comprising the HR Director, the Legal Counsel, and one independent board member. Reports may also be submitted in writing addressed to the HR Director in a sealed envelope marked confidential. For anonymous reporting, a dedicated web-based anonymous reporting form is accessible through the HRMS portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com under the Ethics section. Anonymous reports are accepted and investigated to the extent possible based on the information provided.

**5. Confidentiality**

The identity of the reporting employee will be kept strictly confidential throughout the investigation. Disclosure of the reporter's identity will only occur if required by law or with the explicit written consent of the reporter. All individuals involved in receiving and investigating reports are bound by strict confidentiality obligations. Breach of confidentiality by any investigator or committee member is itself treated as misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy.

**6. Anonymous Reporting**

AkhilaPrime Solutions accepts anonymous reports. While anonymity limits the ability to seek clarification from the reporter, all anonymous reports are reviewed by the Ethics Committee and investigated to the extent the available information allows. Employees who report anonymously are encouraged to include as much factual detail as possible such as dates, names, amounts, and supporting documentation to enable a thorough investigation.

**7. Protection Against Retaliation**

Any employee who raises a concern in good faith under this policy is fully protected from retaliation. Retaliation includes but is not limited to termination, demotion, adverse performance reviews, exclusion from projects, harassment, reduction in compensation, or any other adverse employment action taken as a consequence of the report. Any employee found to have retaliated against a whistleblower will be subject to serious disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment under the Disciplinary Policy. A whistleblower who believes they are being retaliated against must report this immediately to ethics@akhilaprime.com or directly to the HR Director.

**8. Good Faith Reporting**

This policy protects employees who raise concerns honestly and in good faith, even if the investigation ultimately finds no wrongdoing. However, employees who knowingly make false, malicious, or frivolous allegations against a colleague or the company are not protected under this policy and may be subject to disciplinary action.

**9. Investigation Process**

Step 1 is acknowledgment. Upon receipt of a report, the Ethics Committee will acknowledge receipt within 3 working days if the report was not anonymous. Step 2 is the preliminary review. The Ethics Committee will conduct a preliminary review within 10 working days to assess whether the concern falls within the scope of this policy and whether there is sufficient information to proceed with a full investigation. Step 3 is the full investigation. If the preliminary review concludes that a full investigation is warranted, the Ethics Committee will appoint an investigating officer or team. The investigation must be completed within 45 working days of the date the complaint was received, unless the complexity of the matter requires an extension, in which case the reporter will be notified. Step 4 is the outcome. The investigating team will submit a report to the Ethics Committee with findings and recommendations. The Ethics Committee will determine the appropriate action, which may include disciplinary proceedings, financial recovery, legal referral, or process improvements. Step 5 is communication. The reporter will be informed of the outcome to the extent permitted by confidentiality and legal obligations, within 5 working days of the investigation being concluded.

**10. Reporting to Regulatory Authorities**

Nothing in this policy prevents an employee from reporting a concern directly to the relevant regulatory authorities such as the Securities and Exchange Board of India, the Ministry of Corporate Affairs, or any other competent authority, if the employee believes the concern has not been adequately addressed internally or if the law requires direct reporting.

**11. Record Keeping**

All whistleblower reports and investigation records are maintained securely and confidentially by the Ethics Committee. Access is restricted to committee members and legal counsel. Records are retained for a minimum of 7 years from the date of the report.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for reporting genuine concerns promptly and providing as much factual information as possible. The Ethics Committee is responsible for receiving reports, ensuring confidentiality, overseeing investigations, and determining outcomes. The HR Director is responsible for ensuring the policy is communicated to all employees and that anti-retaliation protections are enforced. The Legal Counsel is responsible for assessing legal exposure and advising on regulatory reporting obligations.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by the Ethics Committee in consultation with the Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
