# Rewards and Recognition Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the framework for recognising and rewarding employees at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who demonstrate exceptional performance, embody company values, and make meaningful contributions to the organisation's success. AkhilaPrime Solutions believes that timely, genuine, and consistent recognition is a powerful driver of employee motivation, engagement, and retention. This policy establishes the types of recognition programmes available, eligibility criteria, nomination processes, and the monetary and non-monetary rewards associated with each programme.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all confirmed full-time permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. Probationary employees are eligible for peer recognition and informal recognition but are not eligible for monetary awards until confirmation of employment.

**3. Guiding Principles**

Recognition at AkhilaPrime Solutions is guided by the following principles. Recognition should be timely and given as close as possible to the achievement it celebrates. Recognition should be specific and tied to a clear behaviour, outcome, or value demonstrated by the employee. Recognition should be inclusive and accessible to all employees regardless of team, location, or function. Recognition should be consistent and not influenced by personal relationships or bias. Both managers and peers have a role to play in recognising great work.

**4. Types of Recognition Programmes**

**4.1. Spot Award**

The Spot Award is an immediate, on-the-spot recognition mechanism that any employee including peers and managers can use to acknowledge a colleague's exceptional contribution. Spot Awards are given through the HRMS Recognition Module at hrms.akhilaprime.com and include a personalised message visible to the recipient's team. Spot Awards carry a monetary value of Rs. 1,000 in the form of a digital gift voucher, credited to the recipient's HRMS wallet within 3 working days. Each employee may give a maximum of 2 Spot Awards per month. There is no cap on the number of Spot Awards an employee may receive. Spot Awards are not taxable below the Rs. 5,000 annual threshold per the Income Tax Act. HR will track cumulative Spot Award values and inform employees if they approach the tax threshold.

**4.2. Star of the Month**

The Star of the Month award recognises one employee per department each month who has demonstrated outstanding performance, a significant contribution to a project or client outcome, or exceptional embodiment of AkhilaPrime Solutions' values. Nominations are submitted by managers and peers through the HRMS Recognition Module by the 25th of each month. The department head selects the winner from the nominations received. The Star of the Month is announced in the company-wide Slack channel and featured in the monthly internal newsletter. The award carries a monetary value of Rs. 5,000 in the form of a digital gift voucher and a personalised certificate issued by HR.

**4.3. Quarterly Excellence Award**

The Quarterly Excellence Award recognises employees who have delivered sustained, high-impact contributions over an entire quarter. One award is given per business unit per quarter. Nominations are submitted by managers through the HRMS Recognition Module in the last week of each quarter. A Recognition Committee comprising the HR Business Partner and the respective business unit head selects the winner. The award carries a monetary value of Rs. 15,000 in the form of a digital gift voucher, a trophy, and public recognition at the quarterly all-hands meeting. Recipients are also featured on the company intranet.

**4.4. Annual AkhilaPrime Solutions Champions Award**

The AkhilaPrime Solutions Champions Award is the highest recognition at AkhilaPrime Solutions and is conferred once a year at the Annual Day celebration. Awards are given in the following categories: Tech Innovator of the Year for an employee who has delivered a breakthrough technical contribution; Customer Champion of the Year for an employee who has gone above and beyond to deliver an exceptional client experience; Rising Star of the Year for an employee in their first 2 years at AkhilaPrime Solutions who has demonstrated outstanding potential; Culture Champion of the Year for an employee who has embodied and strengthened AkhilaPrime Solutions' values and culture; and Team of the Year for a team that has delivered an extraordinary collective outcome. Nominations are open to all employees and are submitted through HRMS in February. A panel comprising the CEO, HR Director, and two senior leaders reviews nominations and selects winners. The award carries a monetary value of Rs. 50,000 in the form of a digital gift voucher, a personalised trophy, a certificate signed by the CEO, and a LinkedIn recommendation from the CEO.

**4.5. Long Service Recognition**

AkhilaPrime Solutions recognises the loyalty and commitment of employees who reach service milestones. At 3 years of service, the employee receives a personalised certificate and a gift voucher of Rs. 5,000. At 5 years of service, the employee receives a personalised certificate, a gift voucher of Rs. 15,000, and a feature in the company newsletter. At 10 years of service, the employee receives a personalised certificate, a gift voucher of Rs. 30,000, a trophy, and recognition at the Annual Day event. Long service awards are tracked automatically by HRMS and HR initiates the award process 30 days before the milestone date.

**4.6. Peer-to-Peer Recognition Wall**

AkhilaPrime Solutions maintains a digital Peer-to-Peer Recognition Wall on the company intranet where any employee can post a public appreciation message for a colleague. Recognition Wall posts require no approval and carry no monetary value but are visible to the entire organisation. Employees are encouraged to use the Recognition Wall regularly to build a culture of appreciation. Managers are expected to lead by example by posting at least 2 Recognition Wall appreciations per month.

**5. Nomination and Selection Process**

All monetary recognition programmes require nominations through the HRMS Recognition Module. Nominations must include the employee's name, the award category, a specific description of the achievement or behaviour being recognised, and the business impact where applicable. Self-nominations are not accepted for any monetary award. Selection criteria are based on merit, business impact, and alignment with AkhilaPrime Solutions' values of ownership, collaboration, and continuous improvement. The selection committee for each award is defined in Section 4. Decisions made by the selection committee are final.

**6. Tax Treatment of Awards**

Monetary awards provided through digital gift vouchers are treated as perquisites under the Income Tax Act and are subject to TDS if the aggregate value of non-monetary perquisites received by the employee in the financial year exceeds Rs. 5,000. HR and the payroll team will manage TDS deductions accordingly and reflect the perquisite value in the employee's Form 16.

**7. Recognition Culture Expectations**

Recognition is a shared responsibility at AkhilaPrime Solutions. Managers are expected to make recognition a regular habit, not just an annual exercise. Recognition should be integrated into team meetings, retrospectives, and one-on-one conversations. Lack of recognition for high-performing employees is noted as a leadership gap in manager performance evaluations. HR will track recognition participation rates and share these with department heads quarterly to identify teams where recognition culture needs strengthening.

**8. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for nominating deserving colleagues, participating in recognition programmes, and contributing to the Recognition Wall. The reporting manager is responsible for nominating team members actively, making recognition a regular team practice, and selecting Star of the Month winners. The HR Business Partner is responsible for administering the recognition programmes, tracking participation, processing monetary awards, and reporting on recognition metrics. The HR Director is responsible for overseeing the overall recognition framework and ensuring it remains fair, inclusive, and motivating.

**9. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Award amounts and programme structures may be revised as part of the annual review. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
