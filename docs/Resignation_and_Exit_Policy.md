# Resignation and Exit Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy outlines the procedures, timelines, and responsibilities associated with the voluntary resignation and exit of employees from AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. A smooth and structured exit process ensures continuity of business operations, protects company assets and information, and enables parting employees to receive their full and final settlement in a timely manner.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all permanent and confirmed employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who voluntarily resign from their position. Involuntary separations such as termination for cause are governed separately under the Disciplinary Policy.

**3. Resignation Process**

An employee wishing to resign must submit a formal written resignation letter through the HRMS portal under the Separation module. Physical resignation letters addressed to the reporting manager and HR are also accepted. The resignation letter must include the employee's name, employee ID, designation, department, last working day as per the applicable notice period, and a brief statement of reason for resignation. Verbal resignation is not accepted as a formal resignation. The resignation will be deemed effective only upon written submission and acknowledgment by HR.

**4. Notice Period**

Employees on probation are required to serve a notice period of 1 month. Confirmed employees are required to serve a notice period of 3 months from the date of formal resignation. The notice period begins from the date of resignation submission and acknowledgment by HR. Employees must remain fully productive, committed, and professional throughout the notice period.

**5. Notice Period Buyout**

An employee may request a buyout of the remaining notice period if they are unable to serve the full notice due to a new employer joining date. Notice period buyout is permitted subject to management and HR approval. The buyout amount is calculated as the gross daily salary multiplied by the number of unserved notice days. This amount will be deducted from the employee's full and final settlement. The company may also grant a waiver of notice period at its discretion if a suitable replacement has been identified and knowledge transfer is complete.

**6. Knowledge Transfer**

During the notice period, the resigning employee is required to complete a thorough Knowledge Transfer to their replacement or a nominated colleague. The KT plan must be prepared by the employee and approved by the reporting manager within 5 working days of resignation acceptance. The KT must include documentation of ongoing projects, pending deliverables, client contacts, system access credentials transferred to authorised persons, and any other critical information. The reporting manager must certify the completion of knowledge transfer before the employee's last working day.

**7. Exit Clearance Process**

On the last working day, the employee must complete the Exit Clearance process to ensure all company property is returned and all dues are settled. The clearance involves the following departments. IT must confirm return of company laptop, accessories, ID card, and access card, and must deactivate all system and email access. Finance must confirm no pending salary advances, travel reimbursements, or loan repayments. Admin must confirm return of parking passes, locker keys, and any other company assets. The reporting manager must confirm completion of knowledge transfer and handover. HR must confirm all documentation, full and final settlement computation, and exit formalities. The Exit Clearance form is available in the HRMS portal and must be completed and submitted on or before the last working day.

**8. Exit Interview**

An exit interview will be conducted by the HR Business Partner with every departing employee. The purpose of the exit interview is to understand the reasons for departure, gather feedback on the work environment and management, and identify areas for organisational improvement. Exit interviews are confidential. Participation is strongly encouraged though not mandatory. Exit interview insights are used to improve employee experience and retention strategies.

**9. Full and Final Settlement**

The full and final settlement will be processed within 45 days of the last working day, subject to completion of the exit clearance process. The FnF settlement includes payment of any outstanding salary for days worked, earned leave encashment for balance EL as per the Leave Policy, deduction of notice period buyout if applicable, recovery of any pending advances or loans, gratuity payment if the employee has completed 5 or more years of service, and any other dues or deductions. The FnF amount will be credited directly to the employee's registered bank account. The settlement slip will be shared via the employee's personal email provided during exit.

**10. Gratuity**

Employees who have completed a minimum of 5 years of continuous service at AkhilaPrime Solutions are eligible for gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972. The gratuity amount is calculated as 15 days of last drawn basic salary multiplied by the number of completed years of service. The formula is: Gratuity = (Last Basic Salary divided by 26) multiplied by 15 multiplied by number of completed years of service. Gratuity is paid as part of the full and final settlement.

**11. Experience and Relieving Letter**

Upon successful completion of the exit clearance process and full and final settlement, the employee will receive a Relieving Letter confirming the last working day and a Certificate of Experience confirming the period of employment and designation held. These documents will be issued within 7 working days of FnF processing and sent to the employee's personal email address.

**12. Post-Employment Obligations**

After leaving AkhilaPrime Solutions, the former employee continues to be bound by the Non-Disclosure Agreement signed at the time of joining. Confidential company information, client data, and trade secrets must not be disclosed or used for the benefit of any other organisation. Non-compete restrictions, if included in the employment agreement, will be as specified in the individual employment contract.

**13. Rehire Eligibility**

Former employees who resigned voluntarily in good standing, completed their notice period, fulfilled their KT obligations, and have no outstanding disciplinary issues are eligible for rehire consideration after a minimum gap of 1 year from their last working day. Rehire decisions are subject to business requirements, role availability, and approval by HR and the hiring manager.

**14. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for submitting a formal resignation, serving the notice period, completing KT, and clearing exit formalities. The reporting manager is responsible for accepting or discussing the resignation, overseeing KT completion, and certifying the clearance. HR is responsible for processing the FnF settlement, conducting the exit interview, and issuing experience and relieving letters. Finance and IT are responsible for completing their respective clearance steps within the stipulated timeline.

**15. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR. Amendments will be notified with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any revision.
