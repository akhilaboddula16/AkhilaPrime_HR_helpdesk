# Background Verification Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the background verification process followed by AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. for all new hires prior to or immediately after their joining date. Background verification is a standard practice to ensure that candidates have represented their qualifications, work history, and identity accurately during the recruitment process. It protects the integrity of the organisation, the safety of existing employees, and the trust placed in AkhilaPrime Solutions by its clients. This policy defines the scope of verification, the process, timelines, consequences of adverse findings, and the rights of the candidate or employee during the process.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all individuals offered employment at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. including permanent employees, fixed-term contract employees, and interns. It applies across all bands, departments, and office locations. Third-party contractors and vendors deployed at AkhilaPrime Solutions premises may be subject to background verification as per the terms of their vendor agreement.

**3. Types of Verification Conducted**

AkhilaPrime Solutions conducts the following categories of background verification for all new hires. Identity Verification confirms the candidate's legal name, date of birth, and identity through government-issued documents such as Aadhaar, PAN card, or passport. Address Verification confirms the candidate's current and permanent address through official documents. Education Verification confirms the authenticity of all educational qualifications declared by the candidate including degrees, diplomas, and certifications from the highest qualification downward, typically covering the last two qualifications or more if operationally relevant. Employment Verification confirms the candidate's employment history for the previous 7 years including employer names, designations, dates of joining and relieving, and reasons for leaving as declared by the candidate. Criminal Record Check confirms that the candidate does not have a pending criminal case or conviction that would pose a risk to the company or its clients. In addition, the following checks are conducted for specific roles. Reference Check is conducted for all Band 4 and above positions and involves direct conversation with at least two professional references provided by the candidate. Credit Check is conducted for roles with financial responsibility such as finance, treasury, or procurement roles at Band 3 and above. Global Database Check including sanctions lists and adverse media screening is conducted for all senior leadership hires at Band 7 and above.

**4. Background Verification Agency**

AkhilaPrime Solutions partners with an empanelled third-party background verification agency to conduct all checks. The agency is bound by a non-disclosure agreement and data processing agreement ensuring candidate data is used solely for verification purposes and not retained beyond the period required. Employees do not interact directly with the agency. All communication is routed through the HR team at hr@akhilaprime.com.

**5. Timing of Verification**

Background verification is initiated immediately upon the candidate accepting the offer letter and submitting the required documents. AkhilaPrime Solutions follows a pre-joining verification model where possible, meaning the verification process is initiated before the candidate's date of joining. For roles where business timelines require the candidate to join before verification is complete, the employee is allowed to join conditionally, and the verification is completed within 60 days of the date of joining. The employment during this period is treated as conditional and the offer letter will state this clearly.

**6. Documents Required from the Candidate**

The following documents must be submitted by the candidate through the HRMS onboarding portal at hrms.akhilaprime.com within 5 working days of accepting the offer letter. The documents required are: signed copy of the offer letter, government-issued photo identity proof such as Aadhaar card or passport, PAN card, current address proof not older than 3 months, all educational qualification certificates and mark sheets, relieving letters and experience letters from all previous employers for the last 7 years, last 3 months' salary slips from the most recent employer, and Form 16 or IT returns from the previous employer if requested for senior roles. Failure to submit complete documents within the stipulated time may delay the joining date or result in withdrawal of the offer.

**7. Candidate Rights and Consent**

No background verification will be initiated without the candidate's explicit written consent, which is obtained as part of the offer acceptance process through the HRMS onboarding portal. Candidates have the right to know which categories of checks will be conducted. Candidates have the right to be informed of adverse findings before a final decision is made and to provide their clarification or explanation. Candidate data collected during the BGV process is handled in accordance with AkhilaPrime Solutions' Data Privacy Policy and applicable Indian data protection laws.

**8. Handling of Adverse Findings**

An adverse finding is any discrepancy or negative result discovered during the verification process that differs from what the candidate declared. Adverse findings are classified into two categories.

**8.1. Minor Discrepancy**

A minor discrepancy is a small, non-material difference that does not suggest deliberate misrepresentation. Examples include a difference of a few months in employment dates, a slightly different official job title versus the commonly used title, or a missing certificate for a short-duration non-academic course. In cases of minor discrepancy, HR will contact the candidate or employee to obtain clarification and supporting documents. The employment offer or employment status is not immediately affected, and the discrepancy is resolved through dialogue.

**8.2. Major Discrepancy**

A major discrepancy is a significant or deliberate misrepresentation. Examples include a fabricated degree or certificate, employment dates falsified by more than 6 months, a completely fictitious employer, suppression of a termination for cause, or an undisclosed criminal conviction relevant to the role. In cases of a major discrepancy, HR will issue a Show Cause Notice to the candidate or employee, giving them 5 working days to provide their written explanation. After reviewing the explanation, the HR Director will make a final decision. For candidates who have not yet joined, the offer letter may be withdrawn. For employees who have already joined, the employment may be terminated immediately for misrepresentation, which is treated as gross misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy.

**9. Pending BGV at the Time of Joining**

When an employee joins before the BGV is fully complete, their employment is conditional. The following restrictions apply until BGV is cleared: the employee is not eligible for access to sensitive client data or high-privilege system access, the employee's confirmation is withheld until BGV is cleared, and performance appraisal eligibility may be deferred proportionately. Once all checks are cleared and there are no adverse findings, HR will issue a BGV Clearance confirmation to the employee and update their HRMS profile accordingly.

**10. BGV for Internal Transfers and Rehires**

Employees who are transferred internally to a role with higher security requirements or client data access may be subject to a partial BGV re-check at HR's discretion. Former employees who are rehired are subject to a full fresh BGV regardless of prior verification history.

**11. Confidentiality**

All BGV-related information including documents submitted, agency reports, findings, and decisions is strictly confidential. Access is limited to the HR Business Partner, HR Director, and the relevant department head. BGV results are never shared with the employee's reporting manager in detail. The manager is only informed of whether BGV has been cleared or is pending.

**12. Record Retention**

BGV records are retained in the employee's digital HR file in HRMS for the duration of employment and for 3 years after separation, as required for audit and compliance purposes. Physical copies of identity documents submitted during BGV are not retained beyond the verification period and are destroyed securely.

**13. Roles and Responsibilities**

The HR Onboarding team is responsible for initiating the BGV process, coordinating with the verification agency, collecting documents, communicating with candidates, and maintaining BGV records. The HR Business Partner is responsible for reviewing adverse findings and making recommendations to the HR Director. The HR Director is responsible for making final decisions on adverse findings and approving offer withdrawals or employment terminations arising from BGV outcomes.

**14. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR in consultation with the Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All candidates must consent to background verification as part of the offer acceptance process through the HRMS portal.
