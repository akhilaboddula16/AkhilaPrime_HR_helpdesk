# Leave Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the leave entitlements, procedures, and responsibilities for all employees at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. It is designed to ensure employees can maintain a healthy work-life balance while enabling the organization to plan resources effectively. This policy complies with the Factories Act 1948, Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (amended 2017), and applicable state labour laws governing IT/ITeS establishments in India.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all full-time permanent employees, probationary employees, and confirmed employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. across all offices in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai. Contract staff, third-party consultants, and interns are governed by their respective agreements and are not covered under this policy unless explicitly stated.

**3. Leave Year**

The leave year at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. runs from 1st January to 31st December. Leave balances are credited at the beginning of each calendar year. Employees joining mid-year are entitled to a pro-rated balance based on the number of completed months remaining in the leave year.

**4. Types of Leave and Entitlements**

**4.1. Earned Leave (EL) / Annual Leave**

All confirmed employees are entitled to 18 days of Earned Leave per calendar year. EL is credited in advance on 1st January every year. Employees who join mid-year receive pro-rated EL at the rate of 1.5 days per completed month of service remaining in the year. EL can be availed in a minimum block of 1 day. A maximum of 3 consecutive weeks (15 working days) can be taken at one time. Employees must apply for EL at least 7 working days in advance through the HRMS portal. Approval is subject to manager discretion based on project requirements. Unused EL up to a maximum of 10 days can be carried forward to the next calendar year. Any balance exceeding 10 days at year-end will lapse. Encashment of EL is permitted only at the time of resignation, retirement, or termination at the rate of basic salary divided by 26 multiplied by the number of days encashed.

**4.2. Sick Leave (SL)**

All employees including those on probation are entitled to 12 days of Sick Leave per calendar year. SL is credited on 1st January each year. Pro-rated SL is provided to employees joining mid-year at 1 day per completed month. Sick leave can be availed for personal illness, injury, or medical procedures. For absences of 1 to 2 days, self-declaration via the HRMS portal is sufficient. For absences of 3 or more consecutive days, a medical certificate from a registered medical practitioner must be submitted to HR within 2 working days of resuming duty. Sick Leave cannot be carried forward or encashed. Misuse of sick leave will be treated as misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

**4.3. Casual Leave (CL)**

All employees are entitled to 6 days of Casual Leave per calendar year for urgent personal matters, emergencies, or unforeseen circumstances. CL is credited on 1st January. CL cannot be combined with EL or taken for more than 2 consecutive days. CL must be applied in advance where possible or on the same day of absence through the HRMS portal with manager notification. Unused CL at year-end will lapse and cannot be encashed or carried forward.

**4.4. Maternity Leave**

In accordance with the Maternity Benefit (Amendment) Act 2017, all female employees who have worked for at least 80 days in the 12 months preceding their expected delivery date are entitled to 26 weeks of paid Maternity Leave for the first two children. For the third child onwards, the entitlement is 12 weeks. In case of miscarriage or medical termination of pregnancy, 6 weeks of paid leave is granted from the date of the miscarriage. In case of a tubectomy operation, 2 weeks of paid leave is granted. For adoption of a child below 3 months of age, a commissioning mother is entitled to 12 weeks of Maternity Leave. The employee must submit a written intimation to HR at least 8 weeks before the expected date of delivery along with a medical certificate confirming the expected due date. During maternity leave, the employee is entitled to full pay equivalent to the average daily wage for the preceding 3 months. Maternity Leave cannot be clubbed with any other leave type except EL under specific medical circumstances with HR approval.

**4.5. Paternity Leave**

All male employees and same-sex partners are entitled to 15 days of paid Paternity Leave to be availed within 3 months of the birth or adoption of a child. This is applicable for up to 2 children. The employee must notify HR and the reporting manager at least 4 weeks before the expected date of availing the leave. Paternity Leave can be taken in one continuous stretch or split into two tranches within the 3-month window. It cannot be carried forward or encashed.

**4.6. Bereavement Leave**

Employees are entitled to 5 days of paid Bereavement Leave in the event of the death of an immediate family member. Immediate family includes spouse or partner, children, parents, parents-in-law, and siblings. For the death of a grandparent or close relative not covered above, 2 days of paid Bereavement Leave is granted at the discretion of HR. Employees must notify their manager and HR immediately and submit supporting documentation such as a death certificate upon return to work. Bereavement Leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

**4.7. Menstrual Leave**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. provides 1 day of paid Menstrual Leave per month to all cisgender female employees and transgender employees who menstruate. This leave is in addition to other leave entitlements. No medical certificate or proof is required. The employee must notify their manager by 9:30 AM on the day of availing this leave. Menstrual Leave cannot be accumulated or encashed.

**4.8. Public Holidays**

Employees are entitled to 12 paid Public Holidays per calendar year. The list of Public Holidays is published on the company intranet and HRMS portal at the beginning of each year. Public Holidays include national holidays such as Republic Day (26 January), Independence Day (15 August), and Gandhi Jayanti (2 October), along with regional and festival holidays applicable to each office location. If an employee is required to work on a declared Public Holiday due to business exigency, they are entitled to a compensatory day off to be availed within 30 days of the worked holiday with manager approval.

**4.9. Compensatory Off (Comp Off)**

Employees who are required to work on a weekend or public holiday as directed by their manager are eligible for a Compensatory Off. Comp Off must be applied for within 7 days of working the extra day and must be availed within 30 days of accrual. Comp Off cannot be carried forward beyond 30 days and cannot be encashed.

**4.10. Loss of Pay (LOP)**

If an employee exhausts all available leave balances and is still absent, the additional days will be marked as Loss of Pay. LOP results in a proportional deduction from the monthly salary. Repeated LOP situations may lead to disciplinary review. Employees should notify HR in advance if they anticipate exceeding their leave balances.

**5. Leave Application Process**

All leave applications must be submitted through the AkhilaPrime HRMS portal available at hrms.akhilaprime.com. The employee must select the appropriate leave type, specify dates, and provide a brief reason. The direct manager receives an automated notification and must approve or reject the request within 2 working days. For planned absences such as EL or Paternity Leave, applications must be submitted at least 7 days in advance. For emergency or unplanned absences such as SL or CL, the employee must notify the manager via phone or messaging app before 9:30 AM and submit the HRMS request on the same day or the next working day. Unapplied absences will be automatically marked as LOP after 3 working days.

**6. Holidays During Leave**

Public holidays and weekly offs falling within a period of approved Earned Leave will not be counted as leave days. However, public holidays falling within a period of Sick Leave or Casual Leave will be counted as leave days.

**7. Leave During Notice Period**

Employees who have submitted their resignation are discouraged from availing EL or CL during the notice period except under compelling personal circumstances with prior HR approval. SL during the notice period must be supported by a medical certificate. If leave is availed during the notice period, the notice period will be extended by the equivalent number of leave days taken unless agreed otherwise in writing by HR.

**8. Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for planning leave in advance, applying through HRMS, and ensuring minimum disruption to project deliverables. The reporting manager is responsible for approving or rejecting leave requests within 2 working days, ensuring team coverage during employee absences, and flagging any misuse to HR. The HR team is responsible for maintaining accurate leave records, auditing leave balances, ensuring policy compliance, and handling escalations.

**9. Policy Violations**

Availing leave without approval, misrepresenting the reason for leave, submitting false medical certificates, or taking leave beyond entitlement without LOP acknowledgment will be treated as misconduct under the Employee Code of Conduct Policy and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

**10. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by the Human Resources department in consultation with senior management. Any amendments will be communicated to employees via the company intranet and email with at least 30 days prior notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees are required to read, understand, and acknowledge this policy upon joining AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. and upon any subsequent revision. Acknowledgment is captured digitally through the HRMS portal.
