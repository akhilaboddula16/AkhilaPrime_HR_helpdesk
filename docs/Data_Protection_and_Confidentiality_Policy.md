# Data Protection and Confidentiality Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines how AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. expects its employees to handle, protect, and maintain the confidentiality of company data, client data, and personal data of employees. As a technology company, AkhilaPrime Solutions processes large volumes of sensitive business and personal data in the course of its operations. A breach of data confidentiality can cause irreparable harm to clients, employees, and the company's reputation. This policy establishes the obligations of all employees with respect to data access, handling, storage, sharing, and disposal, and ensures compliance with applicable Indian data protection laws including the Information Technology Act 2000, the IT (Amendment) Act 2008, and the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, consultants, interns, and vendors of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who access, process, or handle any form of company or client data in the course of their work. It covers data in all forms including digital files, databases, emails, printed documents, verbal communications, and any other medium.

**3. Classification of Data**

AkhilaPrime Solutions classifies data into four categories to guide appropriate handling.

**3.1. Public Data**

Public data is information that is intentionally made available to the general public by AkhilaPrime Solutions. Examples include published press releases, marketing materials, job postings, and publicly available product documentation. Public data may be freely shared without restriction.

**3.2. Internal Data**

Internal data is information intended for use within AkhilaPrime Solutions and not meant for external distribution. Examples include internal memos, company announcements, organisational charts, internal process documentation, and general project updates. Internal data may be shared freely within the organisation but must not be disclosed to external parties without authorisation.

**3.3. Confidential Data**

Confidential data is sensitive business information whose disclosure could harm the company, clients, or employees. Examples include client contracts and pricing, product roadmaps, financial forecasts, source code, business strategies, employee compensation details, and vendor agreements. Confidential data must be accessed only on a need-to-know basis and must not be shared externally without explicit authorisation from the department head or Legal team.

**3.4. Restricted Data**

Restricted data is the highest classification and includes data whose unauthorised disclosure could result in legal liability, financial loss, or significant reputational damage. Examples include personally identifiable information of clients and employees, authentication credentials and API keys, payment card data, health and medical records, and data subject to specific contractual or regulatory protections. Restricted data must be encrypted at rest and in transit, accessed only by specifically authorised individuals, and handled in strict compliance with applicable law.

**4. Employee Obligations**

All employees who handle company or client data must comply with the following obligations. Employees must access only the data they are authorised to access for their specific role and must not attempt to access data beyond their access rights. Employees must not share confidential or restricted data with colleagues who do not have a legitimate business need to access it. Employees must not transmit confidential or restricted data through personal email accounts, personal cloud storage, WhatsApp, or any other unsanctioned communication channel. All official communications involving confidential data must use AkhilaPrime Solutions' approved systems such as company email, Slack, and approved cloud platforms. Employees must not store company or client data on personal devices unless specifically authorised in writing by the IT Security team. Employees must not print confidential or restricted documents unnecessarily, and any printed confidential documents must be stored securely and shredded when no longer needed. Employees must not take screenshots, photographs, or screen recordings of confidential or restricted information for personal use or for sharing outside the authorised systems. Employees must use strong passwords and must not share login credentials, access tokens, or API keys with any other person.

**5. Client Data**

Client data is treated with the highest level of protection at AkhilaPrime Solutions. Employees working with client data must access it solely for the purpose of delivering the contracted service. Client data must not be used for any other purpose including testing, demonstration, or training without explicit written consent from the client. Client data must not be transferred out of the designated secure environment without the client's written authorisation and the Legal team's approval. Employees must immediately report any actual or suspected breach of client data to security@akhilaprime.com and the HR Business Partner.

**6. Employee Personal Data**

AkhilaPrime Solutions collects and processes personal data of employees including name, address, bank account details, PAN, Aadhaar, medical information, and performance records for legitimate HR and business purposes. This data is accessible only to authorised HR and Finance personnel on a need-to-know basis. Employee personal data is not shared with third parties except where required by law or for purposes such as payroll processing, insurance, and background verification, in which case appropriate data processing agreements are in place. Employees have the right to access their own personal data held by AkhilaPrime Solutions by submitting a request to hr@akhilaprime.com.

**7. Data Sharing with Third Parties**

No confidential or restricted company or client data may be shared with any third-party vendor, partner, or service provider without a signed Non-Disclosure Agreement and, where applicable, a Data Processing Agreement reviewed by the Legal team. Employees must not share data with third parties based on informal requests or verbal instructions. All third-party data sharing arrangements must be documented and approved by the department head and Legal team.

**8. Data Retention and Disposal**

Data must be retained only for as long as it is required for business or legal purposes. Employees must not retain personal data of clients or employees beyond the period specified in the relevant data retention schedule maintained by the Legal and IT teams. When data is no longer needed, it must be securely deleted or anonymised. Physical documents containing confidential or restricted data must be shredded using a cross-cut shredder and not placed in general waste bins.

**9. Data Breach Reporting**

A data breach is any unauthorised access to, disclosure of, loss of, or destruction of company or client data. All actual or suspected data breaches must be reported immediately to security@akhilaprime.com and the IT Security team, regardless of whether the breach was caused by the reporting employee or by someone else. Employees must not attempt to investigate or resolve a data breach on their own. Early reporting is critical to minimising harm and meeting legal notification obligations under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. Failure to report a known data breach is itself a serious violation of this policy.

**10. Confidentiality Obligations Post-Employment**

Confidentiality obligations survive the end of employment. Former employees must not use, disclose, or exploit any confidential or restricted information acquired during their employment at AkhilaPrime Solutions. Employees sign a confidentiality and non-disclosure agreement as part of their employment contract, and these obligations continue for a period of 3 years after the last working day or indefinitely in the case of trade secrets, whichever is longer. Breach of post-employment confidentiality obligations may result in legal action.

**11. Consequences of Violation**

Violation of this policy is treated as a serious act of misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment. In addition, depending on the nature and severity of the breach, AkhilaPrime Solutions may pursue civil or criminal action against the employee under applicable provisions of the IT Act, Digital Personal Data Protection Act, and the Indian Penal Code.

**12. Training and Awareness**

All employees must complete the Data Security and IT Security training within their first 30 days of joining and must refresh this training annually. The IT Security team will circulate periodic advisories on emerging data security threats and best practices.

**13. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for following all data handling obligations, reporting breaches immediately, and completing mandatory data security training. The IT Security team is responsible for maintaining access controls, monitoring systems for anomalies, responding to data breaches, and providing security training. The Legal team is responsible for ensuring compliance with data protection laws, reviewing third-party agreements, and advising on regulatory obligations. The HR Director is responsible for enforcing this policy and taking disciplinary action in cases of violation.

**14. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by the IT Security team and Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
