# IT Asset and Equipment Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the guidelines for the procurement, allocation, use, care, and return of IT assets and equipment at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. IT assets represent a significant investment by the company and are provided to employees to enable them to perform their work effectively. This policy ensures that company assets are used responsibly, maintained properly, accounted for accurately, and returned in good condition. It also defines employee responsibilities with respect to asset security, acceptable use, and liability in cases of loss or damage.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who are provided with or have access to any company-owned IT asset. IT assets covered under this policy include but are not limited to: laptops and desktop computers, external monitors, keyboards, mice, and docking stations, mobile phones and tablets, headsets and webcams, network and storage devices, software licences, and company SIM cards.

**3. Asset Allocation**

**3.1. Standard Allocation by Role**

All confirmed and probationary employees joining AkhilaPrime Solutions are allocated a company laptop as their primary work device. The standard laptop configuration is determined by the IT team based on the employee's role and technical requirements. The standard allocation per role category is as follows. Software Engineers, Data Scientists, and DevOps Engineers at Band 1 to Band 3 receive a mid-range developer laptop. Senior Engineers and Tech Leads at Band 4 to Band 5 receive a high-performance developer laptop. Architects, Managers, and above at Band 6 and above receive a high-performance laptop with an optional external monitor. Support, operations, and non-technical roles receive a standard business laptop. Additional peripherals such as external monitors, keyboards, headsets, or docking stations may be requested based on role requirements and are subject to manager approval and budget availability.

**3.2. Mobile Phone Allocation**

Company mobile phones are allocated only to employees in roles that require client communication, on-call responsibilities, or frequent travel as determined by the department head and approved by HR. Band 6 and above employees in qualifying roles receive a company mobile phone. Other employees using personal phones for work purposes may apply for a mobile phone reimbursement allowance as defined in the Compensation and Benefits Policy.

**3.3. Work From Home Equipment**

Employees approved for hybrid or full-time Work From Home arrangements are provided with additional equipment to support their home workspace, including an external keyboard, mouse, and webcam if not already allocated. The standard home setup allowance is governed by the Work From Home Policy. Company equipment allocated for home use remains company property and is subject to all the same terms as equipment used at the office.

**4. Asset Onboarding Process**

The IT team is responsible for provisioning all required assets at least 2 working days before the employee's joining date. On Day 1, the IT team or IT helpdesk representative hands over the allocated equipment to the new employee at their workstation or ships it to their home address for remote joiners. The employee must sign an IT Asset Acknowledgment Form in HRMS confirming receipt of each item, including asset IDs, serial numbers, and condition. This acknowledgment must be completed within 3 working days of receiving the equipment. By signing the acknowledgment, the employee accepts responsibility for the equipment and agrees to the terms of this policy.

**5. Acceptable Use**

Company IT assets are provided for work-related purposes. The following uses are permitted: all activities directly related to the employee's job responsibilities, installation of software approved by the IT security team, personal use during non-working hours provided it does not conflict with the IT Security Policy, and use for approved side projects or learning activities if explicitly sanctioned by the manager. The following uses are strictly prohibited: installing unlicensed or pirated software on company devices, using company devices to access illegal content, gambling, or adult websites, using company devices for secondary employment or paid freelance work in violation of the Moonlighting and Conflict of Interest Policy, sharing company devices with family members, friends, or any unauthorised person, bypassing or disabling antivirus, endpoint protection, VPN, or any other security software, storing personal media such as music, photos, or videos on company devices in large volumes, and attempting to access or modify system configurations without IT authorisation.

**6. Employee Responsibilities for Asset Care**

The employee is responsible for keeping assigned equipment in good working condition. The employee must use the provided laptop bag or protective sleeve when transporting the device. The employee must not expose the equipment to extreme temperatures, liquids, or physical impact. The employee must report any malfunction, damage, or loss to the IT helpdesk at it-helpdesk@akhilaprime.com within 24 hours of the incident. The employee must ensure that all company data is backed up to the approved cloud storage or version control systems and must not rely solely on local storage. The employee must keep the device locked with an approved password and must not share login credentials with anyone.

**7. Loss, Theft, and Damage**

**7.1. Accidental Damage**

In the event of accidental damage to company equipment, the employee must report the incident to the IT helpdesk and HR within 24 hours. IT will assess the damage and determine whether it is covered under the company's equipment insurance policy. First-time incidents of accidental damage caused by genuine carelessness will not attract a financial penalty. However, a formal warning may be issued if the damage is found to result from gross negligence. Repeated instances of accidental damage within the same financial year will attract a partial cost recovery from the employee.

**7.2. Loss or Theft**

In the event of loss or theft of company equipment, the employee must file a First Information Report (FIR) with the local police immediately and submit a copy to HR and IT within 48 hours of the incident. All lost or stolen devices will be remotely wiped by the IT team to prevent data breaches. The employee will bear 50 percent of the depreciated replacement cost of the lost or stolen device if it is determined that the loss occurred due to negligence such as leaving the device unattended in a public space. The depreciated value is calculated based on the company's standard asset depreciation schedule.

**7.3. Wilful Damage**

Wilful or deliberate damage to company equipment is treated as misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy and may result in full cost recovery and disciplinary action up to termination.

**8. Software and Licence Management**

All software installed on company devices must be from the approved software list maintained by the IT team. Employees must not install software outside the approved list without prior written approval from the IT team. Approved software requests must be submitted through the IT helpdesk portal at it-helpdesk@akhilaprime.com with a business justification. Software licences assigned to an employee are non-transferable. Upon an employee's exit, all software licences are reassigned or deactivated by the IT team.

**9. Data Security and Device Compliance**

All company devices must have the following security measures active at all times: full-disk encryption enabled by IT, endpoint detection and response software, VPN client for accessing internal systems remotely, screen lock that activates after 5 minutes of inactivity, and regular OS and security patch updates as pushed by IT. Employees may not disable, bypass, or interfere with any of these security measures. Any suspected security breach or compromise of a company device must be reported to IT immediately at it-helpdesk@akhilaprime.com.

**10. Asset Return on Separation**

Upon resignation, termination, retirement, or any other form of separation from AkhilaPrime Solutions, the employee must return all company-owned assets to the IT team on or before their last working day. Assets to be returned include the laptop and all peripherals, mobile phone and SIM card if applicable, ID card, access card, and any other company property. The IT team will inspect the returned assets for condition and completeness and issue an Asset Return Clearance. The Full and Final Settlement will be processed only after all assets have been returned and clearance has been issued by IT. If an employee fails to return assets within 5 working days of their last day, AkhilaPrime Solutions reserves the right to recover the depreciated value of the unreturned assets from the Full and Final Settlement. If the settlement amount is insufficient to cover the recovery, the company may initiate legal proceedings to recover the balance.

**11. Audit and Inventory**

The IT team conducts a physical asset audit at least once per year, typically in January. All employees must cooperate with the audit by presenting their assigned assets for verification. Any discrepancy found during the audit must be explained within 5 working days. Unexplained discrepancies will be treated as asset loss and handled under Section 7.2.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for safeguarding assigned assets, using them appropriately, reporting issues promptly, and returning all assets on separation. The IT team is responsible for asset provisioning, maintenance, security configuration, helpdesk support, and conducting annual audits. The reporting manager is responsible for approving additional asset requests and for ensuring the team follows asset policies. HR is responsible for coordinating asset clearance as part of the exit process and for maintaining asset acknowledgment records in HRMS.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by the IT team and HR. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining, upon receipt of any company asset, and upon any subsequent policy revision.
