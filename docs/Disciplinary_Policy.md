# Disciplinary Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the framework for managing employee misconduct and disciplinary proceedings at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. AkhilaPrime Solutions is committed to maintaining a professional, respectful, and high-performance work environment. When an employee's conduct falls below the expected standards defined in the Code of Conduct Policy or any other company policy, a fair, consistent, and transparent disciplinary process will be followed. The objective of this policy is not merely punitive but corrective — to give employees a clear understanding of where they have gone wrong and an opportunity to correct their behaviour before more serious action is considered.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. including permanent full-time employees, probationary employees, and employees on fixed-term contracts across all office locations and remote or hybrid arrangements.

**3. Principles of Natural Justice**

All disciplinary proceedings at AkhilaPrime Solutions will be governed by the principles of natural justice. No employee will be punished without being informed of the allegation against them. Every employee will be given a fair opportunity to present their version of events and provide their explanation before any disciplinary decision is made. Decisions will be made objectively, without bias, and based on evidence. The severity of the disciplinary action will be proportionate to the severity of the misconduct.

**4. Classification of Misconduct**

Misconduct at AkhilaPrime Solutions is classified into two categories: minor misconduct and major misconduct.

**4.1. Minor Misconduct**

Minor misconduct refers to behaviour that is inappropriate but does not cause immediate or severe harm to individuals, the team, or the company. Examples include but are not limited to: habitual late attendance or unauthorised absence; failure to follow the dress code or general workplace etiquette; use of company equipment for non-work purposes in a non-critical manner; failure to meet deadlines without reasonable cause or prior communication; minor insubordination such as dismissive or disrespectful communication with a colleague or supervisor; and failure to complete mandatory compliance trainings by the stipulated deadline.

**4.2. Major Misconduct**

Major misconduct refers to serious violations that undermine the integrity, safety, or trust within the organisation. Examples include but are not limited to: theft, fraud, or dishonesty involving company property, data, or funds; wilful falsification of records including attendance, expense claims, or performance documentation; physical assault, verbal abuse, or threats against a colleague, manager, vendor, or client; serious violation of the POSH Policy including sexual harassment; wilful damage to company property or data systems; deliberate leakage of confidential company or client information to unauthorised parties; engaging in a serious conflict of interest or unauthorised moonlighting in violation of the Moonlighting and Conflict of Interest Policy; reporting to work under the influence of alcohol or illegal substances; and gross insubordination or wilful refusal to carry out legitimate work instructions.

**5. Disciplinary Procedure**

**5.1. Stage 1 — Verbal Warning**

A verbal warning is the first step in addressing minor misconduct. The reporting manager must have a private, documented conversation with the employee, clearly explaining the nature of the unacceptable behaviour and the expected standard of conduct. The manager must log the verbal warning in the HRMS performance module within 2 working days of the conversation. A verbal warning remains active on the employee's record for 6 months. If no further misconduct occurs within this period, the warning is considered resolved.

**5.2. Stage 2 — Written Warning**

If the same or similar minor misconduct is repeated after a verbal warning, or if a new instance of minor misconduct of greater seriousness occurs, the manager must escalate to HR. A formal written warning letter is issued to the employee by HR, clearly describing the misconduct, referencing the prior verbal warning, and stating the expected improvement. The written warning is uploaded to the employee's profile in HRMS. A written warning remains active for 12 months. A second written warning within 12 months of the first may result in escalation to Stage 3 directly.

**5.3. Stage 3 — Final Written Warning**

A Final Written Warning is issued when a prior written warning has not led to the required improvement, or when a standalone incident of serious minor misconduct warrants it. The Final Written Warning formally notifies the employee that any further misconduct within the next 12 months may result in termination of employment. It is issued in writing by HR in consultation with the HR Director and department head, and requires acknowledgment from the employee within 5 working days.

**5.4. Stage 4 — Termination or Other Major Disciplinary Action**

If an employee's conduct does not improve despite a Final Written Warning, or in cases of confirmed major misconduct warranting immediate serious action, the HR Director in consultation with the department head and the Legal team may initiate the following actions: termination of employment with notice pay as applicable, or summary dismissal without notice or notice pay in cases of gross misconduct as detailed in Section 6. Any action at this stage requires documented evidence, a completed inquiry process, and the HR Director's written authorisation.

**6. Summary Dismissal**

In cases of confirmed gross misconduct, AkhilaPrime Solutions reserves the right to terminate the employee's employment immediately without issuing a prior warning or following the progressive disciplinary steps. Gross misconduct includes but is not limited to: proven fraud, embezzlement, or theft involving the company or its clients; proven physical assault in the workplace; proven sexual harassment after an ICC inquiry; wilful destruction of company or client data; and serious breach of data security or confidentiality. Summary dismissal is approved by the HR Director and is always preceded by a formal inquiry process as described in Section 7.

**7. Formal Inquiry Process**

In cases of alleged major misconduct or any situation that may result in a Final Written Warning, suspension, or termination, a formal inquiry must be conducted before any disciplinary action is taken.

Step 1 is the preliminary fact-finding. Upon receiving a complaint or identifying an instance of potential major misconduct, HR will conduct a preliminary review to determine whether a formal inquiry is warranted. This review is completed within 3 working days. Step 2 is the show cause notice. The employee is issued a Show Cause Notice in writing, clearly stating the allegation, the relevant policy violated, and asking the employee to provide their written explanation within 5 working days. Step 3 is the inquiry committee. HR constitutes an inquiry committee comprising at least 2 members, typically the HR Business Partner and one department head from outside the employee's team, to ensure impartiality. Step 4 is the inquiry meeting. The inquiry committee meets with the employee and relevant witnesses separately. The employee is entitled to present their explanation, call witnesses, and provide documentary evidence. The employee may be accompanied by a colleague of their choice but not by legal counsel or an external representative. Step 5 is the inquiry report. The inquiry committee submits a written inquiry report to the HR Director within 10 working days of the inquiry meeting, with their findings and recommendations. Step 6 is the disciplinary decision. The HR Director, in consultation with the department head, reviews the inquiry report and makes the final disciplinary decision. The decision is communicated to the employee in writing within 5 working days of the inquiry report being submitted.

**8. Suspension Pending Inquiry**

In cases where the alleged misconduct is serious enough to warrant separation from the workplace during the investigation, the HR Director may place the employee on suspension pending inquiry. Suspension pending inquiry is not a punitive measure and does not prejudge the outcome. During suspension, the employee receives their basic salary but is not entitled to allowances. The period of suspension, if followed by exoneration, is treated as paid leave and the full salary difference is settled in the next payroll cycle.

**9. Appeal Process**

An employee who believes the disciplinary action taken against them is unjust or disproportionate may file a written appeal with the HR Director within 5 working days of receiving the disciplinary decision. The HR Director will review the appeal and communicate the outcome within 10 working days. The appeal decision is final. During the appeal period, the disciplinary decision remains in effect unless the HR Director specifically orders otherwise.

**10. Record Keeping and Confidentiality**

All disciplinary records including verbal warning logs, written warnings, inquiry reports, and outcome letters are maintained in the employee's HR file in HRMS and are strictly confidential. Access is restricted to the HR Business Partner, HR Director, and the relevant department head. These records may be used in future disciplinary proceedings or appeals if the employee faces repeated misconduct.

**11. Interaction with Other Policies**

Misconduct related to sexual harassment will be handled under the POSH Policy and investigated by the Internal Complaints Committee. Misconduct related to performance will be managed under the Performance Improvement Plan Process. Conflict of interest violations will be handled under the Moonlighting and Conflict of Interest Policy. The Disciplinary Policy applies in conjunction with these policies where misconduct spans multiple areas.

**12. Roles and Responsibilities**

The reporting manager is responsible for identifying instances of misconduct, conducting verbal warning conversations, logging these in HRMS, and escalating to HR when required. The HR Business Partner is responsible for issuing written warnings, coordinating the inquiry process, and ensuring the disciplinary procedure is followed correctly. The HR Director is responsible for approving all Stage 3 and Stage 4 actions, overseeing inquiry committees, and adjudicating appeals. The department head is responsible for participating in the inquiry process where necessary and providing context on the business impact of misconduct.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR in consultation with the Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
