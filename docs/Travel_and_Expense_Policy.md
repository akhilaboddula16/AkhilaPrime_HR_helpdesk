# Travel and Expense Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy governs the rules and entitlements for business travel and expense reimbursement at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. The objective is to ensure that employees can travel comfortably and safely for business purposes while exercising reasonable cost management. All business travel must be pre-approved and expenses must be actual, necessary, and supported by valid documentation.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who travel on official business including client visits, conferences, training programmes, inter-office travel, and project deployments. Personal travel is not covered under this policy under any circumstances.

**3. Travel Approval**

All business travel must be pre-approved before any bookings are made. Domestic travel requires approval from the reporting manager. International travel requires approval from the department head and HR Director. Travel approval requests must be submitted through the HRMS Travel module at least 5 working days before the travel date for domestic trips and at least 15 working days for international trips. Emergency travel approved verbally must be regularised in HRMS within 24 hours of booking.

**4. Domestic Travel Entitlements**

**4.1. Air Travel**

All domestic air travel must be in economy class regardless of grade or seniority. Business class is not permitted for domestic travel under any circumstance. Flights must be booked through the company's approved travel portal at travel.akhilaprime.com or via the designated travel management company. Employees must book the most cost-effective available option considering travel time constraints. Booking a flight more than 14 days in advance is encouraged to reduce costs.

**4.2. Train Travel**

Train travel for distances exceeding 300 kilometres should be in AC First Class (1A) or AC 2-Tier (2A) based on availability and route. For distances below 300 kilometres, AC 3-Tier (3A) or Executive Chair Car is the standard entitlement. Train tickets must be booked through the company travel portal or IRCTC using the company account.

**4.3. Hotel Accommodation**

Hotel accommodation entitlements are based on the city tier of the travel destination. For Tier 1 cities including Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Hyderabad, Pune, and Chennai, the maximum hotel allowance is Rs. 4,000 per night inclusive of taxes. For Tier 2 cities and other locations, the maximum is Rs. 2,500 per night. Hotels must be booked through the company travel portal wherever available. The hotel must be within reasonable distance from the work location. Luxury hotels and resorts are not permitted unless no standard accommodation is available in the vicinity and prior approval has been obtained.

**4.4. Daily Allowance**

A daily allowance is provided to cover meals, local transport, and incidental expenses during domestic travel. For Tier 1 cities, the daily allowance is Rs. 800 per day. For Tier 2 cities and other locations, it is Rs. 600 per day. Daily allowance is paid for each full day of travel including the day of departure and return if travel extends beyond 12 hours. No receipts are required for daily allowance claims. Daily allowance is not payable when the company provides accommodation and meals directly.

**4.5. Local Transport**

For travel to and from airports, railway stations, and client sites, employees may use taxis booked through approved cab aggregators such as Uber or Ola. Auto-rickshaws, metro, and buses may be used for short distances and are fully reimbursable. Personal vehicle use for official travel is reimbursable at Rs. 8 per kilometre for two-wheelers and Rs. 12 per kilometre for four-wheelers with prior approval.

**5. International Travel Entitlements**

International travel must be approved by the department head and HR Director as described in Section 3. Air travel for international trips up to 6 hours is in economy class. For flights exceeding 6 hours, premium economy class may be booked with department head approval. Business class for international travel requires CEO approval. Hotel accommodation for international travel is reimbursed up to USD 150 per night or equivalent local currency. A foreign daily allowance of USD 75 per day is provided for meals and incidentals. Employees must use the company's forex card issued by the Finance team for international travel expenses. Personal credit card usage is permitted only when the forex card is not usable and must be supported by exchange rate documentation.

**6. Expense Reimbursement Process**

Employees must submit expense claims through the HRMS Expense module within 10 working days of returning from travel. Claims submitted after 30 days of travel will not be reimbursed without written justification approved by HR. All claims must be accompanied by original receipts, invoices, or boarding passes as applicable. Daily allowance claims do not require receipts. Expense claims are reviewed and approved by the reporting manager within 3 working days. Finance processes approved claims within 15 working days of manager approval. Reimbursements are credited directly to the employee's registered salary bank account.

**7. Non-Reimbursable Expenses**

The following expenses will not be reimbursed under this policy: personal entertainment including movies, recreational activities, or sightseeing; alcohol purchases; personal telephone or data charges beyond company-approved limits; minibar or in-room dining at hotels beyond standard meal costs; fines or penalties; personal shopping; and expenses for accompanying non-employees such as family members.

**8. Travel Advances**

Employees may request a travel advance of up to 80 percent of the estimated travel cost through the HRMS Travel module at least 5 working days before departure. The advance will be credited to the employee's salary account. Settlement of the advance must be done within 10 working days of return by submitting actuals in HRMS. Any unutilised advance must be refunded to the company within the same period. Outstanding unsettled advances will result in the withholding of future advances.

**9. Travel Insurance**

AkhilaPrime Solutions provides group travel insurance coverage for all employees on official domestic and international travel. The insurance covers medical emergencies, trip cancellations, loss of baggage, and personal accidents. Employees must carry their company-issued travel insurance card or policy details during all official trips. Claims under travel insurance must be coordinated with the HR and Finance team.

**10. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for planning cost-effective travel, obtaining approvals in advance, maintaining receipts, and submitting claims within the stipulated timeline. The reporting manager is responsible for approving travel and expense claims within 3 working days and ensuring travel is genuinely business-related. Finance is responsible for processing approved claims within 15 working days and maintaining expense records. HR is responsible for policy administration, auditing claims, and addressing exceptions.

**11. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by Finance and HR. Travel entitlement limits may be revised based on inflation and business needs. Changes will be communicated with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any revision.
