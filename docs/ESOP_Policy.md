# Employee Stock Ownership Plan (ESOP) Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. has established an Employee Stock Ownership Plan to provide eligible employees with an opportunity to participate in the company's growth and financial success through ownership of company shares. The ESOP is designed to align employee interests with the long-term interests of the company, reward loyalty and high performance, attract and retain top talent, and foster a sense of ownership and shared purpose. This policy governs the grant, vesting, exercise, and settlement of stock options under the AkhilaPrime Solutions ESOP Scheme 2021.

**2. Scope and Eligibility**

The ESOP Scheme is available to confirmed permanent employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. who have completed a minimum of 12 months of continuous service from the date of confirmation. Employees must be on the company's payroll and in active employment on the date of grant to be eligible to receive an option grant. Directors, consultants, and contract employees are not eligible under this scheme. Employees who are on a Performance Improvement Plan or under disciplinary proceedings at the time of the grant cycle are not eligible for ESOP grants in that cycle. Eligibility is determined by HR and the Compensation Committee in the month of April each year, aligned with the annual appraisal cycle.

**3. Option Pool**

AkhilaPrime Solutions has reserved 8 percent of its fully diluted equity share capital as the total option pool available for ESOP grants. As of the current scheme, the option pool represents approximately 680,000 equity shares. The Compensation Committee and Board of Directors may revise the pool size subject to shareholder approval.

**4. Grant of Options**

Stock options are granted annually in April following the performance appraisal cycle. The quantity of options granted to each eligible employee is based on their performance rating, seniority band, and years of service. The grant structure per appraisal cycle is as follows. Employees rated Outstanding (Rating 5) at Band 1 to Band 3 receive 500 options, at Band 4 to Band 5 receive 1500 options, and at Band 6 and above receive 3000 options. Employees rated Exceeds Expectations (Rating 4) at Band 1 to Band 3 receive 250 options, at Band 4 to Band 5 receive 750 options, and at Band 6 and above receive 1500 options. Employees rated Meets Expectations (Rating 3) are not eligible for ESOP grants in that cycle. The Board may make special one-time grants outside the annual cycle to key hires or critical talent at its discretion.

**5. Exercise Price**

The exercise price is the price at which an eligible employee may purchase AkhilaPrime Solutions shares upon vesting. The exercise price is set at the Fair Market Value (FMV) of the share as determined by a SEBI-registered Category 1 Merchant Banker at the time of each grant. The exercise price for the current grant cycle is Rs. 42 per share. The exercise price is fixed at the time of grant and does not change over the vesting period. The exercise price and current FMV are communicated to eligible employees through their individual ESOP grant letter issued by HR.

**6. Vesting Schedule**

Options vest over a 4-year period from the date of grant following a cliff-and-graded schedule. The vesting schedule is as follows. No options vest in the first 12 months from the grant date. This is known as the cliff period. After 12 months from the grant date, 25 percent of granted options vest at once. Thereafter, an additional 25 percent of the remaining options vest at the end of each subsequent 12-month period. The full vesting schedule is: Year 1 (cliff) is 0 percent, Year 2 is 25 percent, Year 3 is 50 percent, Year 4 is 75 percent, and Year 5 is 100 percent of total options granted. Example: An employee granted 1000 options in April 2024 will have 0 options vested by April 2025, 250 options vested by April 2026, 500 options vested by April 2027, 750 options vested by April 2028, and 1000 options fully vested by April 2029.

**7. Exercise Period**

Vested options may be exercised during the Exercise Period, which begins on the date of vesting and ends 5 years from the date of grant, or upon the occurrence of a Liquidity Event such as an IPO, acquisition, or merger, whichever is earlier. Employees must submit an Exercise Request Form through the HRMS ESOP portal at esop.akhilaprime.com within the exercise window communicated by HR. Options not exercised within the exercise period will lapse permanently.

**8. Liquidity Events**

In the event of a Liquidity Event such as an Initial Public Offering (IPO), merger, acquisition, or strategic sale of AkhilaPrime Solutions, unvested options may be accelerated at the discretion of the Board. In an IPO scenario, all unvested options will vest immediately upon listing. Upon an acquisition or merger, the acquirer may choose to honour existing grants, replace them with equivalent grants in the acquiring entity, or accelerate vesting of unvested options as part of the deal terms. Employees will be notified of specific treatment of their ESOP grants in the event of a Liquidity Event at the earliest opportunity.

**9. Treatment of Options on Separation**

In case of voluntary resignation, unvested options will lapse on the last working day. Vested but unexercised options must be exercised within 90 days of the last working day, failing which they will lapse. In case of termination for cause, all options whether vested or unvested will be forfeited with immediate effect on the termination date. In case of retirement, all unvested options will vest on a pro-rated basis as of the retirement date and may be exercised within 180 days. In case of death or permanent disability, all unvested options will vest immediately and the employee or their legal nominee may exercise them within 180 days of the event.

**10. Taxation**

Under Indian tax law, ESOP taxation occurs at two points. At the time of exercise, the difference between the FMV of the share on the date of exercise and the exercise price paid is treated as a perquisite and is subject to tax deducted at source as part of the employee's salary income. This amount will appear in the employee's Form 16. At the time of sale of shares, any capital gains arising from the sale of shares over the FMV on the date of exercise are subject to capital gains tax, either short-term or long-term depending on the holding period. AkhilaPrime Solutions recommends that employees consult a tax advisor for personalised tax planning related to their ESOP holdings.

**11. ESOP Administration**

The ESOP scheme is administered by the Compensation Committee of the Board of Directors in conjunction with the HR Director and Company Secretary. Employees can view their grant details, vesting status, and exercise history through the ESOP portal at esop.akhilaprime.com. Any queries regarding ESOP grants, vesting, or exercise must be directed to esop@akhilaprime.com. Grant letters are issued digitally through the ESOP portal and must be accepted by the employee within 30 days of issue.

**12. Confidentiality**

ESOP grant details including the number of options, exercise price, and vesting schedule are confidential information. Employees must not disclose their ESOP grant details to colleagues or external parties as this information is commercially sensitive.

**13. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by the Compensation Committee and HR. Any changes to the scheme will be communicated to all eligible employees with adequate notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees receiving an ESOP grant must acknowledge acceptance of the grant letter and the terms of this policy through the ESOP portal within 30 days of receiving the grant letter. Non-acceptance within 30 days will result in the grant being withheld until the next grant cycle.
