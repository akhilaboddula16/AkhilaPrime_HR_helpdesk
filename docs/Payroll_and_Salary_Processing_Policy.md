# Payroll and Salary Processing Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines the payroll processing framework at AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. including salary credit dates, payroll cut-off timelines, salary components, payslip access, tax deductions, and the process for raising and resolving payroll discrepancies. This policy ensures that employees have a clear and complete understanding of how, when, and in what form their salary is processed and paid, and what steps to take if they notice an error. AkhilaPrime Solutions is committed to accurate, timely, and transparent payroll processing for all employees.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all full-time permanent employees and fixed-term contract employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. across all bands, departments, and office locations.

**3. Salary Credit Date**

Salary for each calendar month is credited to the employee's registered bank account on or before the last working day of that month. In months where the last working day falls on a public holiday or weekend, salary is credited on the preceding working day. For new joiners whose joining date falls after the payroll cut-off date defined in Section 4, the salary for the partial month of joining is processed along with the following month's payroll. New joiners are informed of this at the time of onboarding.

**4. Payroll Cut-Off Date**

The payroll cut-off date is the 20th of every month. All inputs affecting the salary for that month must be submitted to the payroll team by the 20th. This includes attendance records and approved leaves, any approved allowance or reimbursement claims, bank account change requests, income tax declaration updates, and any other payroll-impacting changes. Inputs received after the 20th will be processed in the following month's payroll. Employees are encouraged to ensure all attendance, leave, and reimbursement records are accurate and up to date in HRMS before the 20th of each month.

**5. Salary Components**

The salary structure at AkhilaPrime Solutions comprises the following components. Basic Salary forms 40 percent of the employee's gross CTC and is the foundation for statutory calculations including PF and gratuity. House Rent Allowance is 20 percent of the gross CTC and is partially exempt from income tax subject to actual rent paid and applicable Income Tax Act provisions. Special Allowance is 20 percent of the gross CTC and is fully taxable. Variable Pay is 15 percent of the gross CTC and is paid subject to individual performance rating and company performance, as defined in the Compensation and Benefits Policy. Employer Provident Fund Contribution is 5 percent of the gross CTC and is deposited directly to the employee's EPF account with the EPFO. The net take-home salary is the gross CTC minus employee-side statutory deductions including employee PF contribution at 12 percent of basic salary, professional tax as applicable by state, and income tax deducted at source based on the employee's tax regime and declarations.

**6. Statutory Deductions**

The following statutory deductions are made from the employee's monthly salary. Employee Provident Fund deduction is 12 percent of the basic salary, deposited to the employee's EPF account. Professional Tax is deducted as per the applicable slab in the employee's state of employment. Income Tax is deducted at source every month based on the employee's projected annual income, tax regime chosen, and approved investment declarations submitted through HRMS. Any other statutory deduction mandated by applicable Indian law.

**7. Income Tax and Investment Declarations**

At the beginning of each financial year in April, employees must submit their income tax investment declarations through the HRMS Payroll section. This includes declarations of proposed investments under Section 80C such as PPF, ELSS, and LIC premiums, house rent paid for HRA exemption, home loan interest and principal for applicable deductions, and any other exemptions or deductions the employee intends to claim. The payroll team uses these declarations to compute the monthly TDS. Employees must submit actual proof of investment by January 31st of the same financial year. If actual proof is not submitted, TDS will be recalculated based only on confirmed investments and may result in higher deductions in the remaining months of the financial year. Employees may update their tax declarations at any time during the year through HRMS, subject to the payroll cut-off date.

**8. Choice of Tax Regime**

Employees may choose between the Old Tax Regime and the New Tax Regime under the Income Tax Act 1961 at the beginning of each financial year. The chosen regime is applied for TDS computation for the entire financial year. Employees who wish to change their tax regime for the following year must update their selection in HRMS during the April declaration window. The payroll team will communicate the deadline for tax regime selection each year. Employees are encouraged to consult a tax advisor to determine which regime is more beneficial based on their individual financial situation.

**9. Payslip Access**

Monthly payslips are generated and made available to employees through the HRMS Payroll section by the 5th of the following month. Employees can access, download, and print their payslips at any time through HRMS. Payslips are password-protected with the employee's date of birth in the format DDMMYYYY. For any issues accessing payslips, employees may contact hr@akhilaprime.com. Annual Form 16 is issued to all employees by June 15th of the following financial year through HRMS.

**10. Bank Account for Salary Credit**

Salary is credited only to the employee's bank account registered in HRMS. Employees must ensure their bank account details are accurate in HRMS at all times. To update or change a bank account for salary credit, the employee must submit a Bank Account Change Request through the HRMS Payroll section, attaching a cancelled cheque or bank account statement as proof. Bank account changes submitted before the 20th of the month will be effective from that month's payroll. Changes submitted after the 20th will be effective from the following month. Salary will not be credited to third-party accounts under any circumstances.

**11. Reimbursements**

Approved reimbursements such as travel and expense claims, medical reimbursements, and phone or internet allowances are processed as part of the regular monthly payroll cycle, provided they are submitted and approved in HRMS before the payroll cut-off date of the 20th. Reimbursements submitted after the cut-off date are processed in the following month's payroll. Reimbursement amounts are shown as a separate line item in the payslip and are generally non-taxable subject to applicable conditions and submission of bills.

**12. Salary for Partial Month**

Salary for a partial month, applicable in cases of new joining, resignation, or any approved unpaid period, is calculated on a per-day basis using the formula: monthly gross salary divided by the number of calendar days in that month, multiplied by the number of working days in that month for which the employee was on the payroll.

**13. Salary Discrepancy Resolution**

If an employee notices an error or discrepancy in their salary, they must raise a Payroll Query through the HRMS Payroll section within 30 days of the payslip being issued. The payroll team will investigate and respond within 5 working days of the query being raised. If the discrepancy is confirmed, the correction will be processed in the next payroll cycle along with any arrears due. If an employee was overpaid due to a payroll error, AkhilaPrime Solutions will recover the overpaid amount in the next payroll cycle after informing the employee. For complex discrepancies involving multiple months, a recovery plan will be agreed upon with the employee to avoid excessive deductions in a single month. Employees may also write directly to hr@akhilaprime.com for payroll queries if they are unable to raise the query through HRMS.

**14. Salary on Hold**

Salary may be placed on hold in the following circumstances. Salary for an employee who has not completed KYC or bank account registration in HRMS will be held until the information is submitted. Salary for an employee whose background verification has resulted in an adverse finding and whose employment status is under review may be held at the discretion of the HR Director pending the outcome. In cases of suspected payroll fraud, the Finance and HR teams may place an employee's salary on hold pending investigation. Employees will be informed in writing if their salary is placed on hold, with the reason and expected timeline for resolution.

**15. Full and Final Settlement**

The Full and Final Settlement payout for separating employees is processed within 45 days of the last working day, subject to completion of the exit clearance process. The FnF payslip is issued through HRMS and reflects all dues and deductions including outstanding salary, earned leave encashment, notice period recovery or payment, outstanding advances, and any other adjustments. Gratuity, if applicable, is paid separately in accordance with the Payment of Gratuity Act 1972, within 30 days of the last working day.

**16. Confidentiality of Salary Information**

Salary is strictly confidential information. Employees must not disclose their salary details to colleagues. Managers must not disclose the salary of their team members to others. The payroll team must not share individual salary information with any unauthorised person. Breach of salary confidentiality is treated as misconduct under the Disciplinary Policy.

**17. Payroll Calendar**

The payroll team publishes an annual Payroll Calendar at the start of each financial year in April, listing the cut-off dates, salary credit dates, and reimbursement submission deadlines for all 12 months. The Payroll Calendar is available on the HRMS Payroll section and is also shared on the company intranet.

**18. Roles and Responsibilities**

The employee is responsible for keeping bank account details updated in HRMS, submitting investment declarations and proofs on time, logging attendance and leave accurately before the cut-off date, raising payroll queries within 30 days of the payslip, and maintaining confidentiality of salary information. The payroll team is responsible for processing salary accurately and on time, generating payslips, handling queries, managing statutory compliance, and issuing Form 16. The HR Business Partner is responsible for coordinating payroll inputs such as new joiner and leaver information, leave data, and approved allowances. The Finance team is responsible for fund disbursement, statutory remittances, and audit compliance.

**19. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR and Finance. Any amendments including changes to salary credit dates or cut-off dates will be communicated to all employees with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
