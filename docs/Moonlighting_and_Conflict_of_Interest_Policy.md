# Moonlighting and Conflict of Interest Policy

**1. Policy Brief and Purpose**

This policy defines AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd.'s position on moonlighting, dual employment, freelance engagements, and conflict of interest situations. The policy ensures that employees maintain undivided professional commitment to the company, protect confidential information, and avoid situations where personal or financial interests conflict with their responsibilities to AkhilaPrime Solutions.

**2. Scope**

This policy applies to all full-time permanent and probationary employees of AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. across all locations and work arrangements including remote and hybrid.

**3. Definition of Moonlighting**

Moonlighting refers to an employee taking up a second job, freelance project, contract engagement, or any form of paid work outside of their employment with AkhilaPrime Solutions, whether during or outside of official working hours. This includes but is not limited to: taking up employment with another organisation, providing paid consultancy or advisory services, developing and commercially deploying software products, engaging in paid content creation related to technology or business, and participating as a paid director or trustee of another commercial entity.

**4. AkhilaPrime Solutions' Position on Moonlighting**

AkhilaPrime Solutions Pvt. Ltd. does not permit employees to take up secondary employment or paid engagements that directly or indirectly compete with the company's business interests, involve the use of company resources, data, or intellectual property, require the employee to work during official working hours, cause a decline in the employee's primary job performance, or create a conflict of interest as defined in Section 6. Employees found to be engaged in unauthorised moonlighting will face disciplinary action up to and including termination.

**5. Permitted Exceptions**

The following activities are permitted without prior approval: unpaid volunteering for registered NGOs or educational institutions, part-time teaching or guest lecturing at academic institutions without compensation or with a nominal honorarium not exceeding Rs. 5,000 per month, personal creative pursuits such as writing, art, or music that have no connection to the company's business or clients, and investment in publicly listed securities as a personal investor without any advisory or management role. The following activities are permitted with prior written approval from HR and the reporting manager: paid teaching or training at academic institutions, writing books or technical articles, participating as a speaker at conferences with compensation, and serving on advisory boards of non-competing organisations. Approval requests must be submitted to hr@akhilaprime.com with full details of the engagement.

**6. Conflict of Interest**

A conflict of interest arises when an employee's personal, financial, or external professional interests could impair or appear to impair their ability to act in the best interests of AkhilaPrime Solutions. Situations that constitute a conflict of interest include: having a direct financial interest in a competitor, client, or vendor of AkhilaPrime Solutions; conducting business with a company in which a family member holds a significant position; making procurement or hiring decisions that benefit a personal contact or family member; receiving gifts, payments, or benefits from clients or vendors beyond the limits set in the Code of Conduct Policy; and accepting a position on the board of a competitor organisation.

**7. Disclosure Obligation**

Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to HR in writing as soon as they become aware of it. The disclosure form is available in the HRMS portal under the Compliance section. Failure to disclose a known conflict of interest is itself a violation of this policy. Upon receiving a disclosure, HR will assess the situation in consultation with the department head and Legal team and will communicate the appropriate course of action within 10 working days. Disclosure does not automatically mean the situation is permissible but ensures transparency.

**8. Consequences of Violation**

Employees who engage in unauthorised moonlighting or fail to disclose conflicts of interest will be subject to disciplinary action. Depending on the severity and nature of the violation, consequences may include formal written warning, recovery of salary paid during the period of concurrent employment, suspension without pay pending investigation, or termination of employment. In cases where confidential company information has been misused or disclosed to a competitor or client, legal action may also be initiated.

**9. Company's Right to Investigate**

AkhilaPrime Solutions reserves the right to conduct investigations into suspected moonlighting or undisclosed conflicts of interest. Employees must cooperate fully with any such investigation. Refusal to cooperate will be treated as a separate act of misconduct.

**10. Roles and Responsibilities**

Employees are responsible for disclosing all secondary engagements and conflicts of interest proactively and honestly. Managers are responsible for monitoring team behaviour and flagging suspected moonlighting or conflicts to HR. HR is responsible for reviewing disclosures, investigating violations, and maintaining confidentiality of all disclosures.

**11. Policy Review**

This policy is reviewed annually by HR in consultation with the Legal team. Any amendments will be communicated with 30 days advance notice.

**Acknowledgment**

All employees must acknowledge this policy through the HRMS portal upon joining and upon any subsequent revision.
